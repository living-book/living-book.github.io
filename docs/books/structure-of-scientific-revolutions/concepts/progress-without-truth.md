# Progress Without Truth

## Definition

Kuhn closes the book by separating two things usually fused: instrumental progress (undeniable — later paradigms solve more puzzles with more precision than earlier ones) and teleological progress ("closer to the truth," which he argues does no explanatory work and should be relinquished). His explicit model is Darwinian: as Darwin removed the goal from biological evolution, Kuhn removes the goal from scientific development. Paradigms are selected for puzzle-solving fitness within an environment of anomalies, the way organisms are selected for reproductive fitness — with irreversibility guaranteed but no destination implied.

## In the Book

Kuhn asks why science looks uniquely progressive and answers partly sociologically: victors write the textbooks (the invisibility mechanism of Chapter 11), but mostly it's the community's structure — insulation from lay demands, shared rigorous training via exemplars, evaluation only by expert peers, generational renewal through education — that produces a field which reliably gets better at solving its own problems. He states the reversal directly: "we may have to relinquish the notion... that changes of paradigm carry scientists... closer and closer to the truth," while insisting the developmental process is still genuine "evolution from primitive beginnings," just not evolution "toward anything."

## Why It Matters

This gives a way to affirm that a field, practice, or organization is genuinely getting better without having to defend a claim about a final correct state it's approaching — useful wherever "the right architecture" or "the final answer" talk quietly smuggles in an unjustified destination. The tradeoff it names honestly: a paradigm's fitness is relative to its current environment of anomalies, so what counts as progress today can expire as the environment shifts, which is a sharper and more falsifiable claim than either naive cumulativism or the absence of any standard at all.
