# Paradigm Crisis

## Definition

An anomaly is a violation of paradigm-induced expectation — and paradoxically it takes a rigid, precise framework to make violations visible at all: "novelty ordinarily emerges only for the man who, knowing with precision what he should expect, is able to recognize that something has gone wrong." When anomalies accumulate, resist the field's best attacks, and strike at fundamentals, confidence erodes community-wide into crisis. Crisis is the necessary precondition for new theory, but not sufficient cause for abandoning the old one: "to reject one paradigm without simultaneously substituting another is to reject science itself."

## In the Book

Kuhn traces discovery as a process rather than a moment through oxygen — Priestley collected the gas but categorized it within phlogiston chemistry as "dephlogisticated air," while Lavoisier, primed by mounting anomalies in weight-gain experiments, saw a genuinely new substance and rebuilt chemistry around it — and through Roentgen's X-rays, where a glowing screen that "shouldn't" glow, judged anomalous only against cathode-ray expectations, triggered weeks of investigation. Crisis proper is illustrated by Copernicus inheriting a Ptolemaic astronomy he called a "monster," patched endlessly and stalling calendar reform, and by phlogiston chemistry drowning in the same weight-gain anomalies before Lavoisier's response. Chapter 8 adds the crisis-behavior typology: most astronomers kept patching epicycles (rationally, since patching had always worked before), while others turned to first principles and wild alternatives — the rule-loosening that crisis, and only crisis, permits.

## Why It Matters

The concept separates two things routinely conflated: noticing a problem and that problem becoming decisive. It gives anomaly detection a precondition (precise expectations, not vague hopes) and gives framework-abandonment a precondition (a working alternative, not just accumulated failure) — which means "this is broken" is a complaint, and only "this alternative solves what breaks here" is a decision. That reframing applies to any domain where an old system persists past its obvious flaws until a viable replacement, not a critique, finally displaces it.
