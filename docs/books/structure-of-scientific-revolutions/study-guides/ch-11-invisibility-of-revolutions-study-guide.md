# Study Guide: Chapter 11 — The Invisibility of Revolutions

## Core Concept
Textbooks rewrite history after every revolution, casting the past as linear approach to the present. The cumulative image of science is manufactured by pedagogy — functional for training, corrosive for judgment.

## Key Terms
Textbook science · invisibility · functional distortion · Orwell analogy

## Case Summary
Dalton's fixed proportions: textbooks present it as an experimental discovery; historically, atomism forced a redefinition of "compound" that made the law true — a hidden revolution.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Locate what your org's official history smooths over
- [ ] Preserve raw records: memos, dead options, actual arguments
- [ ] Before a big bet, consult the unsanitized version of the last one

## Self-Test
1. Why don't science students read primary sources — and what follows?
2. How is the distortion functional rather than conspiratorial?
3. What's the cost of believing your field's linear origin story during a crisis?
