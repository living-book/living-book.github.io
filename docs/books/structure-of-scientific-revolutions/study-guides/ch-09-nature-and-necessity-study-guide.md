# Study Guide: Chapter 9 — Nature and Necessity of Revolutions

## Core Concept
Revolutions are noncumulative: the new paradigm is incompatible AND incommensurable with the old — different problems, standards, meanings. Debates are circular; persuasion, not proof, decides. Politics is the model.

## Key Terms
Incommensurability · noncumulative change · circularity · revolution

## Case Summary
Einstein doesn't extend Newton: deriving Newtonian equations changes what mass, space, and time mean. The "special case" is a different ontology wearing familiar symbols.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] In your current big debate, list each side's success criteria — check for circularity
- [ ] Identify terms that changed meaning between camps ("quality", "done")
- [ ] Design a demonstration-that-recruits instead of another argument

1. Why can't normal rules settle paradigm debates?
2. In what precise sense is Newton NOT a special case of Einstein?
