# Study Guide: Chapter 4 — Normal Science as Puzzle-Solving

## Core Concept
Normal problems are puzzles: solution assured, rules fixed, failure blames the solver not the theory. During normal science, theories test scientists — Popper inverted.

## Key Terms
Puzzle · assured solution · rules (explicit/implicit) · commitments

## Case Summary
Nineteenth-century atomic-weight refinement and constant-measurement: nobody doubted the framework; the challenge — and the career — was in the craft.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Label current problems: puzzle vs open question — assign accordingly
- [ ] When something fails, check calibration: skill-blame vs framework-blame
- [ ] Write down three unstated rules constraining your acceptable solutions

## Self-Test
1. What makes a problem a puzzle regardless of importance?
2. Why is it rational to blame the practitioner during normal science?
3. Where does Lakatos's "hard core" echo this chapter?
