# Study Guide: Chapter 12 — The Resolution of Revolutions

## Core Concept
Paradigm contests end by conversion cascade: early adopters commit on promise and aesthetics, produce visible wins, arguments multiply, the profession tips, holdouts are outlived. Reasons without rules; Planck's principle does the rest.

## Key Terms
Conversion · Planck's principle · five values · early-adopter faith

## Case Summary
Priestley never accepted oxygen chemistry; early Copernicans converted on mathematical harmony before telescopic evidence. Both rational — on different value-weightings.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Route change through early adopters and demonstrations, not memos to skeptics
- [ ] Audit your resistances: reasons or tenure?
- [ ] Size early framework bets as faith-plus-promise — that's the actual game

## Self-Test
1. Why must early adopters decide "in defiance of the evidence"?
2. The five values — and why their variable weighting is functional?
3. What distinguishes Kuhn's account from "mob psychology"?
