# Study Guide: Chapter 6 — Anomaly and Discovery

## Core Concept
Discovery is a process, not a moment: awareness of anomaly → extended exploration → adjusted categories. Precise expectations are the anomaly-detector; rigidity makes surprise possible.

## Key Terms
Anomaly · paradigm-induced expectation · discovery-as-process · perceptual assimilation

## Case Summary
Oxygen: Priestley isolates it but sees "dephlogisticated air"; Lavoisier, primed by weight anomalies, sees a new element and rebuilds chemistry. Roentgen's X-rays: a glow that shouldn't exist, chased for weeks.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Make one expectation precise enough that violation would register
- [ ] Pick the anomaly you've been calling noise; give it three hours
- [ ] Grant someone explicit license to chase their glowing screen
