# Study Guide: Chapter 18 — The Leader's New Work

## Core Idea
Learning-organization leaders are designers (building systems and integrated learning processes), stewards (holding a vision in service of a larger purpose story), and teachers (helping people see systemic structure and purpose, not just events). Their core task is generating and holding creative tension across the whole organization.

## Key Terms
Leader as designer/steward/teacher · purpose story · visionary crisis manager vs. visionary strategist · creative tension (organizational scale)

## Case Summary
Joanne's preemptive limits-to-growth fix that "dissolves" a problem before it's ever visible as a crisis. Bill Gore's controlled-growth story (25%/year cap, "57.5 years to have everyone in the world working for Gore"). Lyndon Johnson's Great Society vision destroyed by lying about Vietnam War costs — the "credibility gap."

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Ask whether a recent "leadership win" was crisis heroics or invisible design work that prevented the crisis
- [ ] Check whether your organization's vision is backed by a purpose story, or is just a stated goal
- [ ] Notice whether leadership communication operates mostly at the event level, or reaches systemic structure and purpose

## Self-Test
1. Why is "leader as designer" the most important but least credited leadership role, per the ocean-liner exercise?
2. What's the difference between a "visionary crisis manager" and a "visionary strategist," and why do both fall short?
3. Why did Lyndon Johnson's leadership fail despite genuine vision and enrollment skill?
