# Study Guide: Chapter 3 — Prisoners of the System, or Prisoners of Our Own Thinking?

## Core Idea
Structure influences behavior: identical system structures (the MIT beer game) produce the same boom-bust crisis regardless of who plays. Real causes sit at the level of systemic structure, not individual event-level decisions or blame.

## Key Terms
Systemic structure · beer game · three levels of explanation (events/patterns/structure) · vicious cycle of overordering · "take two aspirin and wait"

## Case Summary
One flat step-change in consumer demand (4→8 cases/week) produces retailer orders past 24, wholesaler orders near 40 truckloads, brewery production near 80 gross — then gluts of 100-220 units nobody wants. "No strategy" (just reorder what came in) beats 75% of real teams. FDR's 1933 banking-holiday speech models a true structural explanation.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] When a metric spikes, ask if you're seeing a one-time step change amplified by delay, not a real ongoing surge
- [ ] Before reordering/re-escalating, check what's already "in the pipeline" but not yet arrived
- [ ] Push your own explanation of a recurring problem past "who did what" to "what structure makes this pattern likely"

## Self-Test
1. Why do thousands of different beer-game players all produce the same crisis pattern?
2. What are the three levels of explanation, and why is only the structural one generative?
3. What are the two guidelines that cut beer-game costs to roughly a tenth of typical scores?
