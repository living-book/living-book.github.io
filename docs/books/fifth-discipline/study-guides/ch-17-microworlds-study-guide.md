# Study Guide: Chapter 17 — Microworlds: The Technology of the Learning Organization

## Core Idea
Microworlds compress time and space so teams can experience the consequences of decisions that would otherwise be too distant to learn from directly — solving the "dilemma of learning from experience." They combine team-dynamics learning with real strategic-complexity learning.

## Key Terms
Microworld · transitional object · prediction vs. forecast (Pierre Wack)

## Case Summary
Index Computer's $2B sales plan collapses once modeled explicitly — rookie/veteran productivity mix drags growth below $1.5B, echoing People Express. Meadowlands Shelving's price-vs-service impasse: discounting produces a reinforcing decline; investing in service produces a 2-year dip then a turnaround by year 3, because customers need years to experience better service before valuing it.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Before committing to a growth plan, model the implicit assumptions (e.g., steady productivity) explicitly and stress-test them
- [ ] When two people hold opposite, well-evidenced views, check whether they're both right on different time horizons
- [ ] Look for a low-risk simulation or pilot to run a contested strategy forward before committing fully
