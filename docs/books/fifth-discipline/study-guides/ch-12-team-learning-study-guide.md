# Study Guide: Chapter 12 — Team Learning

## Core Idea
Teams are the fundamental learning unit in organizations. Team learning rests on distinguishing dialogue (free exploration toward shared meaning) from discussion (advocacy aimed at winning), and on facing defensive routines rather than being derailed by them.

## Key Terms
Alignment · dialogue vs. discussion (Bohm) · suspending assumptions · colleagueship · defensive routines (Argyris) · practice fields

## Case Summary
Bill Russell's Celtics' "alignment" — individual energies harmonizing like laser light. DataQuest Drives' two-day dialogue session (explicit ground rules: suspend assumptions, act as colleagues, spirit of inquiry) surfaces a decade-long R&D/marketing split neither side could name alone. Management teams lack the "practice fields" that basketball teams, orchestras, and ad agencies use to rehearse before performing for real.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Before a contentious meeting, name explicitly whether you need dialogue (explore) or discussion (decide)
- [ ] Watch for defensive routines (smoothing over vs. "abstraction wars") and treat their intensity as a signal of what matters most
- [ ] Look for a low-stakes way to "practice" a decision (simulation, small pilot, dry run) before committing for real
