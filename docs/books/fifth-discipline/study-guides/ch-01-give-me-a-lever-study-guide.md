# Study Guide: Chapter 1 — Give Me a Lever Long Enough… and Single-Handed I Can Move the World

## Core Idea
Learning organizations continually expand their capacity to create desired results, built on five disciplines (systems thinking, personal mastery, mental models, shared vision, team learning) that must converge as one ensemble — not applied separately.

## Key Terms
Learning organization · metanoia (shift of mind) · the five disciplines · generative vs. adaptive learning · component technologies

## Case Summary
The DC-3 succeeded where the Boeing 247 failed by fusing five component technologies at once; missing just one (wing flaps) made the 247 unstable. Shell's 1970s–80s turnaround (weakest of seven majors to strongest) came from treating planning as institutional learning — surfacing and revising shared mental models continuously.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Name which of the five disciplines your team/org is missing, not just which it's strong in
- [ ] Check whether "vision" work is grounded in systems thinking, or floating free of current reality
- [ ] Ask: is this adaptive learning (coping) or generative learning (expanding capacity to create)?

## Self-Test
1. Why did the DC-3, not the Wright Flyer, start commercial aviation — and what's the analogy to the five disciplines?
2. What does metanoia mean, and how does it differ from "taking in information"?
3. Why is systems thinking called the "fifth" discipline rather than just one of five?
