# Study Guide: Chapter 16 — Ending the War Between Work and Family

## Core Idea
Work/family imbalance follows the "Success to the Successful" archetype: two reinforcing loops (work success, family success) competing for one limited resource — time — structurally unstable and biased to drift toward work. Fixing it requires stepping outside the structure with an explicit personal vision and goals, not just trying harder.

## Key Terms
Success to the Successful (archetype) · covenant vs. contract (recap) · creative tension applied to time allocation

## Case Summary
Fortune's 1990 finding: 36% of executives' children in psychiatric/drug treatment vs. 15% for non-executives, blamed solely on parents. Bill O'Brien's claim that learning-organization leadership skills are the same skills as effective parenting. Only 51.5% of management-position families had a stay-at-home spouse by the book's writing.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Diagram your own work/family time allocation as two competing reinforcing loops before trying to "just balance better"
- [ ] Set concrete, specific personal time goals (nights home, meeting limits) rather than a vague intention to balance
- [ ] Check whether your organization treats work/family as an acceptable topic in real time-commitment discussions

## Self-Test
1. Why is "Success to the Successful" inherently unstable rather than self-correcting?
2. What three factors bias the structure to drift toward work rather than family?
3. Why does Senge say trying to "balance better" from inside the structure usually fails?
