# Chapter 19 — A Sixth Discipline?

## Core Thesis

The DC-3 (Ch. 1) started commercial aviation, but the industry didn't become major until two more technologies converged over a decade later — the jet engine and radar, the latter a wartime by-product, not aviation research at all. Senge's closing hedge on his own framework: the five disciplines form a critical mass sufficient to make building learning organizations systematic rather than accidental, but they are probably not the last word.

## The Shift

Just as jet engines and radar built the infrastructure (airports, pilots, mechanics, airlines) that made the DC-3's breakthrough into a real industry, the immediate task is mastering the five disciplines as they stand — establishing the foundation on which some future, currently unimaginable discipline might be built, likely arriving from an unlikely direction, the way radar arrived from war research rather than aeronautics.

## Key Terms

- **Critical mass of disciplines** — the claim that five specific disciplines, together, are sufficient (not necessarily final) for systematic learning-organization building
