# Study Guide: Chapter 3 — Forms of Superintelligence

## Core Idea
Three forms — speed (faster), collective (many aggregated), quality (qualitatively smarter) — share equal indirect reach (any could build the others) but differ in direct reach. Machine substrate advantages (speed, communication latency, scale, editability, duplicability) are so large they guarantee digital minds outclass even enhanced biological ones.

## Key Terms
Speed superintelligence · collective superintelligence · quality superintelligence · direct reach · indirect reach · copy clan

## Case Summary
Proust's *In Search of Lost Time*: no assemblage of "literary hacks," however large, substitutes for one mind of the right quality — illustrating that some problems are solvable only by quality superintelligence, not by scaling collective labor.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Separate "faster/more of the same" gains from "different in kind" gains when assessing a system's power
- [ ] Don't conflate collective intelligence with wisdom — a system can optimize brilliantly and still miss the one thing that matters
- [ ] Ask whether a problem is decomposable/parallelizable (favors collectives) or requires unified deep understanding (favors quality)
- [ ] When comparing biological vs. engineered systems, check orders-of-magnitude substrate gaps, not just current performance

## Self-Test
