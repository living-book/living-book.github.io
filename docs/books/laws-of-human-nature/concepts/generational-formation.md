# Generational Formation

## Definition

Every person is shaped by the specific economic, technological, and cultural conditions of the moment they were formed in, and absorbs the resulting assumptions not as one possible framework among many but as simply "the way things are" — natural, obvious, and universal. This isn't a claim about ignorance; it's a structural feature of how formative experience gets encoded: the parent shaped by scarcity doesn't announce a belief in financial conservatism, they simply live it in a thousand daily choices, and the child absorbs it as reality before having the tools to question it. The result is that responses adaptive to one historical context get carried, unexamined, into a different one where they no longer fit — and the carrier experiences the mismatch as the world being wrong, not their framework being outdated.

## In the Book

Law 17's exemplar is Benjamin Franklin, whose 84-year life spanned colonial subject to revolutionary leader to founding statesman, and who — unlike most contemporaries — studied these historical transitions rather than merely living through them. The book's clearest illustration is his French diplomatic mission (1778–1785): Franklin understood that French support for the Revolution was rooted in a century-long rivalry with Britain, not sympathy for American ideals, and he deliberately performed the "American" persona — plain dress, fur hat, unpretentious manner — because he recognized its specific cultural resonance in a French court chafing against aristocratic formality. The book contrasts this with contemporaries like Massachusetts governor Thomas Hutchinson, who could not imagine successful colonial separation because he was fully inside the assumptions of his moment rather than able to see them as one possible arrangement among others.

## Why It Matters

This gives a mechanism for a specific, recurring failure: mistaking a moment-bound framework for a permanent truth, then reading anyone operating under different assumptions as wrong rather than differently formed. It supplies a concrete tell — confusion or moral offense at how a different generation, culture, or era operates is often diagnostic of an unexamined assumption rather than a genuine ethical violation — which is useful anywhere path-dependent mental models get mistaken for objective reality: organizations inheriting a founder's-era playbook, incumbents blindsided by a shift they can only see as "doing it wrong," or any system built by people who no longer notice which of their premises are load-bearing versus merely historical.
