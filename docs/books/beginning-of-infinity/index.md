# The Beginning of Infinity — Companion

David Deutsch's 2011 argument that the growth of knowledge is **unbounded** — and that we stand at its infancy, not its culmination. From the criterion of good explanations (hard to vary) to the multiverse, the nature of persons, and the fragility of the Enlightenment, Deutsch builds one sweeping case: anything not forbidden by the laws of physics is achievable with the right knowledge.

## Master Themes

- **Hard-to-vary explanations** — the signature of real knowledge, with unbounded reach
- **Universal explainers** — people can achieve anything knowledge permits
- **Conjecture and criticism** — the sole engine of knowledge, in genes and minds alike
- **Optimism, precisely** — problems are inevitable, and problems are soluble
- **The dynamic society** — a rare, fragile tradition of criticism worth defending
