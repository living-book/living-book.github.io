# Study Guide: Chapter 16 — The Evolution of Creativity

## Core Idea
Creativity evolved to *transmit* culture faithfully, not to innovate. Acquiring complex tribal memes requires creatively reconstructing the hidden rules behind others' behavior. Its original survival value was high-fidelity conformity; only the Enlightenment redirected it toward innovation.

## Key Terms
Creativity for transmission · meme acquisition · redirected creativity · static-society paradox

## Case Summary
A child masters tribal norms by conjecturing their unstated rules — the same explanatory creativity later used for science. Static societies then bent it toward reproducing anti-rational memes.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] See deep learning as creative reconstruction, not passive absorption
- [ ] Ask if your team's creativity reproduces "how we do things" or improves them
- [ ] Surface the hidden rules you conjectured to master your craft — then criticize them

## Self-Test
1. Resolve the paradox: creativity arising in change-hostile societies.
2. Why does faithful cultural learning require creativity?
3. What did the Enlightenment do to this faculty?
