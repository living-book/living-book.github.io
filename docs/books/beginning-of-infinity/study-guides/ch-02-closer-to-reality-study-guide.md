# Study Guide: Chapter 2 — Closer to Reality

## Core Idea
All perception is theory-laden inference; instruments are just better inference chains. Processed telescope imagery is *more* direct than the naked eye, not less. Reality of an entity depends on explanation quality, not chain length.

## Key Terms
Theory-laden observation · virtual-reality chamber · inference chain · observable/unobservable (dissolved)

## Case Summary
Deutsch "sees" a galaxy: every photon hitting his retina came from the screen, none from the galaxy — yet the observation is more faithful than any unaided look.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Audit the theories embedded in your key metrics pipeline
- [ ] Give "I saw it myself" the same scrutiny as a dashboard
- [ ] Trust instrument over gut when the inference chain is better-criticized

## Self-Test
1. Why is instrumented observation more direct, not less?
2. What is the "virtual-reality chamber"?
3. How does this dissolve the observable/unobservable line?
