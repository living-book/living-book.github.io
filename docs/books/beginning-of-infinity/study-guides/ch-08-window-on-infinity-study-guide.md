# Study Guide: Chapter 8 — A Window on Infinity

## Core Idea
Infinity is a disciplined working environment, not a mystery. Hilbert's Infinity Hotel trains the intuition: "full" never means "no vacancy." We are always at the *beginning* of infinity — "running out" arguments smuggle in unearned finitude.

## Key Terms
Infinity Hotel · beginning of infinity · measure problem · countable infinity

## Case Summary
The hotel absorbs new guests, infinite coaches of guests — by shuffling. But some tasks stay impossible and there's no uniform "random room": infinite ≠ anything-goes.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Find a "running out of ideas" claim that assumes a finite bound
- [ ] Sort your constraints: which are unbounded (conjecture-space) vs bounded (compute, attention)
- [ ] Be optimistic about the unbounded, arithmetic about the bounded

## Self-Test
1. Why doesn't "full" mean full in the Infinity Hotel?
2. What is the measure problem?
3. What does "beginning of infinity" mean for present stakes?
