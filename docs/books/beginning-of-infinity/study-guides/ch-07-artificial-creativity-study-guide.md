# Study Guide: Chapter 7 — Artificial Creativity

## Core Idea
An AGI would be a **person** — a universal explainer with full moral status — and can't be built by stacking tricks or mimicking outputs. It requires a theory of creativity (how new explanations arise) that nobody has. The Turing test judges outputs and so can't distinguish understanding from mimicry.

## Key Terms
AGI as person · Lovelace's objection · mimicry vs origination · creativity (unsolved)

## Case Summary
Lovelace: the Engine "originates nothing." Deutsch sides with her deep point — surprise isn't origination; recognizing AGI means explaining its mechanism, not judging behavior.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] For an AI tool, design a test distinguishing understanding from mimicry in that domain
- [ ] Separate genuine capability-stacking value from extrapolation into an unmade jump
- [ ] Watch for the tell: a system that "disobeys well" — new criticism and problems
