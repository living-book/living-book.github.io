# Study Guide: Chapter 10 — A Dream of Socrates

## Core Idea
A dialogue: knowledge comes from conjecture criticized, not authority or the senses. All communication is conjectural — listeners *guess* meanings and err productively. Even a god can't pour knowledge in; teaching helps the learner create it.

## Key Terms
Fallibilism · conjectural communication · productive misunderstanding · Socratic method (recast)

## Case Summary
Hermes teaches Socrates fallibilism; young Plato misunderstands and writes down authoritative Forms — the most consequential productive misunderstanding in history.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Treat every handoff as a conjecture-generator; add teach-back loops
- [ ] Harvest interesting misreadings — they may be creations
- [ ] Find the "Forms" in your org — an authority beyond criticism

## Self-Test
1. Why can't knowledge be transmitted, only reconstructed?
2. How does the Plato frame prove the dialogue's own thesis?
3. What replaces authority at every level of the dialogue?
