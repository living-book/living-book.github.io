# Study Guide: Chapter 14 — Why Are Flowers Beautiful?

## Core Idea
Some beauty is objective. A flower signaling to a different species — no shared genes, no shared code — must embody hard-to-fake, hard-to-vary criteria. Human cross-cultural love of flowers taps that same objective beauty. Beauty can be discovered, not just assigned.

## Key Terms
Objective beauty · cross-species signaling · parochial vs universal appeal · hard-to-fake signal

## Case Summary
Flower and pollinator solve a communication problem across an unbridgeable genetic gap — only objective standards of attractiveness work reliably.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Separate parochial fashion from quality that survives an audience gap
- [ ] Stop defaulting to "it's all subjective" and seek objective quality
- [ ] Test a signal across the widest gap you can; what survives is closer to objective
