# Study Guide: Chapter 6 — The Jump to Universality

## Core Idea
Systems built for parochial ends cross a discontinuous threshold to **universality** — doing everything in their domain (alphabets, numerals, computers, DNA). The jump is usually unintended and unnoticed by its makers; digitality and error-correction travel with it.

## Key Terms
Universality · the jump · parochialism · error-correction · digitality

## Case Summary
Babbage's Analytical Engine: universal computation in brass, unrecognized even by its builders. Genetic code: universal for eons before organisms exploited its reach.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Find a system in your stack that jumped but is still used parochially
- [ ] Spot deliberate non-universality (Roman-numeral choices) in your field
- [ ] Ask what your universal tools can do that nobody requested

## Self-Test
1. Why does the jump happen "as an unintended consequence"?
2. Why do universality and digitality travel together?
3. What's the AI relevance of jump-vs-gradual thinking?
