# Study Guide: Chapter 5 — The Reality of Abstractions

## Core Idea
Abstractions (numbers, ideas, morals) are real: they figure indispensably in the best explanations, and reality's criterion is explanatory. Reductionism ("only microphysics causes") is false; emergence has autonomous, causally potent levels.

## Key Terms
Emergence · explanatory criterion of reality · reductionism (rejected) · levels of explanation

## Case Summary
Domino computer: an output domino stays up because **641 is prime**. The microphysical story explains nothing; the abstraction does the causal work — so primality is real.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Find a problem you keep fixing at the wrong level of emergence
- [ ] Name an abstraction (trust, debt, morale) your best explanation requires — treat it as real
- [ ] Catch "it's just X underneath" hiding the actual cause

## Self-Test
1. Why does the prime-domino show abstractions cause physical effects?
2. What is the explanatory criterion of reality?
3. Why is reductionism false as an explanatory doctrine?
