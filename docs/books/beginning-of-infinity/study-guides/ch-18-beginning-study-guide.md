# Study Guide: Chapter 18 — The Beginning

## Core Idea
We are at the *beginning* of infinity: the Enlightenment began the first sustained tradition of criticism and the open-ended growth of knowledge. This is history's infancy, not its culmination — unbounded, but fragile, and abortable if the tradition of criticism isn't defended.

## Key Terms
Beginning of infinity · dynamic society · the choice · tradition of criticism

## Case Summary
Synthesis of the book: the momentous dichotomy, the rarity of dynamic societies, and the choice — sustain criticism or drift to stasis. Progress is unlimited but not guaranteed.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Treat your field as at its beginning, not its maturity
- [ ] Identify which conditions of criticism you're transmitting vs letting decay
- [ ] Resist the pull toward a "stable end-state" when dynamism is the safe course

## Self-Test
1. Why is our era a beginning rather than a culmination?
2. What makes the dynamic society fragile?
3. What is "the choice" Deutsch leaves the reader?
