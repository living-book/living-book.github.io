# Concept 4 — The Hard Core

## Core Thesis

Every research programme has a **hard core**: the central commitments its adherents decide, methodologically, to treat as irrefutable. The negative heuristic forbids directing refutations at it — anomalies must be deflected into the auxiliary belt. This "dogmatism" is not a vice; it is what gives a programme identity and staying power. Abandon the core and you haven't revised the programme; you've left it.

## The Problem It Solves

Why tenacity is rational. If practitioners renegotiated foundations at every anomaly, no deep consequences would ever be derived — the programme would die of doubt before its power showed. The hard core is a division of intellectual labor across time: fix the foundations *by decision*, and spend the community's energy where adjustment actually pays.

## Key Episode

Prout's programme in chemistry (1815): hard core — all atomic weights are whole-number multiples of hydrogen's. Measured weights disagreed constantly (chlorine: 35.5). Proutians didn't touch the core; they attacked the belt — purification techniques, measurement theory — for a century. Vindication came absurdly late: isotopes explained the anomalies, and the core stood essentially right. A century of "dogmatism" paid off.

## The Shift

From beliefs-held-because-true to commitments-held-by-decision. The core's irrefutability is *conventional* — chosen, not proven. That single move dissolves Kuhn's mystery of dogmatic normal science into strategy: protecting foundations is a policy with a rationale, revisable at the level of programme choice.

## Critiques & Rivals

The obvious danger: the same structure shields astrology and pseudo-science — their cores also deflect refutation. Lakatos's answer is that protection is licensed only while the programme *progresses* (predicts novel facts); degenerating programmes protect cores too, but irrationally. Critics reply that this only relocates the problem — and note real cores do erode historically, piece by piece.

## Modern Application

Name your hard core explicitly — the two or three commitments your team will not renegotiate this year — and route all criticism to the belt around them. Teams without a declared core relitigate identity in every retro; teams with one convert criticism into adjustment energy. But calendar the core review: protection is a policy, and policies expire on evidence of degeneration.

## Key Terms

- **Hard core** — the programme's conventionally irrefutable center
- **Negative heuristic** — the rule deflecting refutation from the core
- **Methodological decision** — irrefutability by choice, not proof

## Key Quotes

> "The negative heuristic of the programme forbids us to direct the modus tollens at this 'hard core'."

> "We must use our ingenuity to articulate or even invent 'auxiliary hypotheses', which form a protective belt around this core."

## Reflection Questions

1. What is your programme's hard core — and is its protection a conscious decision or an unexamined habit?
2. Which anomaly are you wrongly aiming at someone's core, guaranteeing defensive war?
3. When is your next scheduled core review — and what evidence would trigger revision?
