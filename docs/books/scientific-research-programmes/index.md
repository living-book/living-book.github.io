# The Methodology of Scientific Research Programmes — Companion

Imre Lakatos's answer to the Popper–Kuhn war: science is rational, but the unit of rationality is not the theory — it's the **research programme**, a long campaign with a protected hard core, an adjustable belt, and a battle plan. Progress means predicting novel facts; degeneration means explaining yesterday. This companion is organized by concept rather than by the book's dense paper-structure.

Each concept page includes an audio overview, a start infographic (read before), and a shift infographic (read after).

## The Architecture

**Hard core** (protected by the negative heuristic) → **protective belt** (absorbs refutation) → **positive heuristic** (plans the belt's evolution) → scored as **progressive or degenerating** over time.

## Master Themes

- **Trajectory over snapshot** — everything is anomaly-ridden; growth is the test
- **Refutation requires a rival** — grievances don't kill frameworks, alternatives do
- **Protection is a policy** — tenacity as strategy, with an expiry condition
- **Novel facts are the currency** — a few stunning predictions outweigh oceans of accommodation
- **Rationality runs slow** — honest scorekeeping, not instant verdicts
