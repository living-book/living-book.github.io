# Hard Core

## Definition

The hard core is the small set of central commitments a research programme's practitioners decide, by convention rather than proof, never to abandon in response to anomalies. The negative heuristic is the accompanying rule: "forbids us to direct the modus tollens at this 'hard core'" — refutations must be deflected into the adjustable belt around it. Abandoning the core doesn't revise the programme; it ends it and starts a different one.

## In the Book

Lakatos's key episode is Prout's hypothesis in 19th-century chemistry: the hard-core claim that all atomic weights are whole-number multiples of hydrogen's, against measured weights (chlorine at 35.5) that disagreed constantly. Proutians spent a century attacking the protective belt — purification techniques, measurement theory — never the core. Vindication arrived absurdly late, when isotopes explained the discrepancies and the core turned out essentially right. The book presents this as proof that "dogmatism" toward a core can be rational rather than merely stubborn — but only conditionally: protection is licensed while the programme keeps producing novel predictions (progressing), and the same shielding structure that protects Newton's laws also protects astrology's core, so the hard core alone cannot do the demarcating work; that job falls to progressive versus degenerating problemshifts (see that concept).

## Why It Matters

This concept converts "why won't they update their beliefs?" from a character flaw into a legible policy question. Treating foundational commitments as chosen-to-be-fixed rather than believed-because-proven explains why sustained, focused effort under known anomalies can be the rational choice rather than denial — a team that renegotiates its foundations at every setback never accumulates the depth needed to see whether the foundations were right. The concept also supplies a diagnostic: name the two or three commitments a team, theory, or organization will not renegotiate, route criticism there deliberately, and — crucially — schedule when that protection itself gets reviewed, since the license expires on evidence of degeneration.
