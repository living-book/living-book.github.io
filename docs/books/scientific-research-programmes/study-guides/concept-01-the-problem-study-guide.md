# Study Guide: Concept 1 — Rescuing Rationality

## Core Concept
Popper's logic fails history (great science ignored refutations); Kuhn's history seems to abandon logic ("mob psychology"). Lakatos's project: rationality that fits the actual record — by appraising series of theories over time, not single theories at moments.

## Key Terms
Demarcation · naive falsificationism · rational reconstruction · trajectory appraisal

## Case Summary
Newton's programme: born refuted, lived in anomalies, most successful ever. Any rulebook that condemns it condemns itself.

## Application Checklist
- [ ] Recast one "is X dead?" debate as a trajectory question
- [ ] List a framework's last three versions before judging it
- [ ] Separate today's embarrassments from the growth curve

## Self-Test
1. What does Popper get wrong about history? Kuhn about rationality?
2. Why must appraisal target series of theories?
3. What was at stake in demarcation for Lakatos?
