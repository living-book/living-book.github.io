# The Power of Silence

## Definition

The Power of Silence is the deliberate use of silence as a coaching tool to shift the locus of problem-solving from the leader to the team. A good ScrumMaster says what needs to be said. A great one knows when *not* to speak, holding space for the team to think, reflect, and come up with its own solution. This works because people who answer their own questions and generate their own solutions are more empowered and more likely to succeed.

The core mechanism: silence creates discomfort. In most Western cultures (around 9-10 seconds in the UK, longer in Scandinavia), that discomfort drives someone to break the silence—and when the ScrumMaster doesn't, the team member must. This forces reflection and ownership.

## In the Book

Watts tells the story of Ossie, a ScrumMaster who deliberately arrived late to the daily scrum to see if the team would run it without him. The team waited. Confused, they asked where he'd been. Ossie didn't explain; he just posed a question and then waited. The awkward silence built. Eventually Perry hypothesized that Ossie had done this intentionally to test their autonomy. Ossie confirmed it with another question, not an explanation.

The principle: don't break the silence. Different cultures have different comfort thresholds with quiet, but eventually someone will speak. That's almost always preferable to the ScrumMaster breaking the silence, because it forces the team to reflect and self-organize. This reinforces the lack of authority in the ScrumMaster role and teaches teams they can function without the ScrumMaster present.

Watts notes that good ScrumMasters expose the "elephant in the room." Great ones know that the collective problem-solving skills of the team are stronger and more conducive to team bonding than any solution the ScrumMaster proposes. So they say what needs to be said, then leave space—silence—for the team to think, act, and solve on its own.

## Why It Matters

Over-helping is a trap of leadership. When a leader immediately answers questions, proposes solutions, or fills every gap, they teach a team to defer, not to think. The Power of Silence is the inverse: it's a tool for building team capacity, autonomy, and cohesion. It works across domains wherever a leader needs to develop others' capability while stepping back from authority. Teams that develop through silence become self-managing.
