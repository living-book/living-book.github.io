# Culture as the Medium for Goals

## Definition

Doerr's claim is not that OKRs create culture, but that they require one to function: "Like sound waves, [goals] require a medium... For OKRs and CFRs, the medium is an organization's culture, the living expression of its most cherished values and beliefs." OKRs carry leaders' priorities; CFRs transmit them person to person; but neither can substitute for shared values, psychological safety, or trust — they only work efficiently inside a culture that already has those things, and can help build that culture when it's absent.

## In the Book

Chapter 18 grounds the claim in Andy Grove's own writing in High Output Management: an "intelligent corporate citizen" who has internalized a culture's values "will behave in consistent fashion under similar conditions," sparing managers the inefficiency of enforcing everything through formal rules. Doerr pairs this with Google's internal Project Aristotle, a study of 180 teams that found standout performance correlated with five factors — structure and clarity, psychological safety, meaning of work, dependability, and impact — and argues the first (structure and clarity) is literally what OKRs exist to supply, while the rest are what CFRs and healthy culture supply. Chapter 19 (the Lumeris story) shows OKRs and CFRs deployed explicitly as a culture-change intervention at a health-care company facing internal resistance, and chapter 20 (Bono's ONE Campaign) shows the same tools used to instill accountability culture in an advocacy nonprofit operating across dozens of countries.

## Why It Matters

A measurement or goal-setting technique transplanted into a system without the underlying trust and shared values to support it will produce compliance theater rather than results — the numbers get filled in, but nothing actually improves. Recognizing that a tool needs a medium to propagate through is a check against the common mistake of assuming a mechanism (a KPI dashboard, a voting system, an incentive scheme) will work identically regardless of the culture it's dropped into.
