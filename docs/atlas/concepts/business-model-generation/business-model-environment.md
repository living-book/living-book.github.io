# Business Model Environment

## Definition

A business model doesn't exist in a vacuum — it is designed and executed inside a "design space" defined by design drivers (new customer needs, new technologies) and design constraints (regulatory trends, dominant competitors). The book proposes mapping that space across four areas: market forces (customer needs, market segments, switching costs, revenue attractiveness), industry forces (competitors, new entrants, substitute products, suppliers and value-chain actors, stakeholders), key trends (regulatory, technology, societal/cultural, socioeconomic), and macroeconomic forces (global market conditions, capital markets, commodities, economic infrastructure).

## In the Book

The chapter argues that continuous environmental scanning matters more than ever given networked business models, technology-driven uncertainty, and market disruptions from new value propositions — and frames a strong environmental model as what lets an organization "become a shaper and transformer" of its environment rather than merely react to it. It works the pharmaceutical industry through all four force categories as a running example: market forces surface skyrocketing healthcare costs and a shift from treatment to prevention; industry forces cover customer segments like doctors, regulators, distributors, and patients; and the chapter poses explicit questions per category (e.g., "What are the crucial issues affecting the customer landscape? Which shifts are underway?") rather than leaving the mapping abstract.

## Why It Matters

A canvas alone can make a model internally coherent while blind to the forces that will break it — a strong value proposition means nothing if a regulatory trend or a new entrant is about to invalidate the assumption underneath it. Forcing four separate sweeps (market, industry, trend, macro) instead of one generic "environmental scan" catches different failure modes: industry forces catch competitive threats, key trends catch slower-moving shifts an org might otherwise dismiss as noise, and macro forces catch shocks no single competitor controls.
