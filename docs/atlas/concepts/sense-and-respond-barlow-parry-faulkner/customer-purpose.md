# Customer Purpose

## Definition

Customer purpose is the underlying outcome or goal that customers aim to achieve when they use a product or service. It goes beyond stated needs or specifications. A customer doesn't want a drill—they want holes in a wall. Understanding customer purpose requires seeing the transaction from the customer's perspective and the context in which they use what you provide.

## In the Book

The book emphasizes distinguishing between "what matters" and "what's important" to customers. Barlow, Parry, and Faulkner illustrate this through asking fundamental questions: Why are customers using our products? What function do they serve? Do they improve the customer's working or personal environment?

The authors argue that organizations often fail to grasp customer purpose because they see the world from inside-out rather than outside-in. Most traditional approaches ask "How can we sell what we make?" rather than "What does the customer ultimately need to accomplish?" For example, a tire repair shop might focus on "fixing tires quickly" but miss the true customer purpose: "enabling the customer to reach their destination safely and on time." Once this deeper purpose is understood, the organization can innovate beyond the original service offering.

## Why It Matters

Understanding customer purpose unlocks innovation and reveals hidden market opportunities. It shifts the organization from defending its current product-service mix to actively seeking ways to deliver the customer's desired outcome more effectively. This reorientation changes how you measure success—not by delivery speed or specification compliance, but by whether the product or service enabled the customer to achieve their actual purpose. It also creates a mechanism for continuous adaptation: frontline workers who understand customer purpose can flag when customer contexts change or when competitors better serve that purpose.
