# Freed Capacity

## Definition

When process-time reduction eliminates wasted effort, the result is "freed capacity" — hours of human time no longer needed for that task. The book insists this is categorically not the same thing as headcount to be cut: "it's the work that's non-value-adding, not the people." Organizations that get sustained Lean transformation right make an up-front, explicit commitment that eliminating work will not result in eliminating workers.

## In the Book

Chapter 4 states the mechanism plainly: process-time reductions free capacity, and "if freeing capacity results in a decision to lay off people, there will be unintended negative consequences" — employee interest in further improvement will plummet, and no future value stream effort will succeed. Because labor expense is often 80 percent or more of the budget in office and service environments, the book frames freed capacity as the central lever for growing revenue-to-expense ratios without proportional hiring — but only if it's reinvested, never cut. The sidebar "What to Do with Freed Capacity" lists productive uses: absorb additional work without adding staff, reduce overtime or temp/contract staffing, in-source outsourced work, reduce hours for better work/life balance, slow down to do higher-quality and safer work, innovate and build new revenue streams, run continuous improvement, and deepen customer and supplier relationships. The one carve-out: if layoffs are truly unavoidable (e.g., a market collapse), the book says to do them *before* starting a transformation that depends on people feeling safe enough to expose problems and propose changes — and if reductions become unavoidable mid-journey, leadership must explicitly and visibly attribute them to market conditions, never to the improvement work itself.

## Why It Matters

Any system that asks people to expose the waste in their own jobs is asking them to work against their own interests unless that risk is explicitly and credibly removed in advance. The commitment to redeploy rather than eliminate isn't a soft HR nicety bolted onto the method — it's the precondition that makes honest current-state discovery possible at all, in this book or in any domain where the people who can see the waste are the same people whose roles it touches.
