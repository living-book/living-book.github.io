# Reason Without Cause

## Definition

Explanation and causation are not the same relation. A cause produces its effect through some prior efficient act; a reason (ratio) accounts for why something is so or why an agent wills something, without that account being a productive act at all. This distinction lets philosophers explain a self-existing or uncaused first principle without contradicting themselves by making it the efficient cause of its own existence.

## In the Book

Wippel's chapter on Aquinas (Ch. 4) builds directly on this move. Asked why God creates at all, Aquinas holds that God's goodness is the *reason* God wills other things to exist, but explicitly not the *cause* of that willing — Wippel traces this to Aquinas's own text (Summa Contra Gentiles I, c. 86–87), which "introduces an interesting distinction between a *ratio* and a cause." Pressed further — why does God himself exist? — Wippel argues Aquinas would say God's essence is identical to his act of existing, so God necessarily exists; this, Wippel concludes, "is in fact for him to offer a reason (*ratio*) for God's existence, but not a cause," since "there is, in the case of his existence, a reason without an efficient cause."

Tad Schmaltz's chapter on Descartes (Ch. 5) shows the same distinction under strain. Descartes wants a "cause or reason" for everything, including God, and initially calls God *causa sui* — cause of himself. Pressed by Arnauld's objection that nothing can efficiently cause itself, Descartes retreats: God stands to his own existence only "in some manner [*quodammodo*]" as an efficient cause stands to its effect, invoking a formal-cause analogy instead (*quasi causa efficiens*). Schmaltz reads Descartes's final position, despite the strained terminology, as landing in the same place as Aquinas: a reason for God's existence that is not an (efficient) cause of it.

## Why It Matters

Any explanatory chain that must terminate — a first cause, an axiom, a founding assumption — faces the same problem: the terminus can't be explained the same way as everything downstream of it without infinite regress or self-causation. "Reason without cause" is the move that lets a chain stop without becoming either arbitrary or self-caused: the terminus is accounted for, just not produced. The same shape shows up wherever an explanation needs to bottom out — in why a formal system's axioms hold, or why a foundational value in a design is what it is — without smuggling in a hidden earlier cause.
