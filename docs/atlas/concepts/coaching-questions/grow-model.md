# The GROW Model

## Definition

GROW is a four-step conversational sequence — **G**oal, **R**eality check, **O**ptions, **W**ill — that moves a person from a fuzzy desire to a committed next action within a single conversation. Each step has a distinct job: define what "done" looks like, establish the objective facts of the starting point, generate several possible paths, then convert the chosen path into a specific, high-buy-in action step.

## In the Book

Stoltzfus presents GROW as "the most widely used conversational structure in coaching," useful even in encounters as short as ten minutes, and cites John Whitmore's *Coaching for Performance* as its origin. He is precise about where each step can go wrong: the Reality step fails when the coach asks for subjective self-evaluation ("How often do you usually exercise?") instead of a concrete fact ("How many times did you exercise in the last week?"); the Options step fails when the coach supplies suggestions instead of making the coachee do the generative work; the Will step fails when a coach accepts a low-confidence commitment — he has the coach ask "On a scale of one to ten, how likely is it that this step will get done?" and troubleshoot any answer under eight rather than let a doomed action step stand. He also scopes the model's limits: GROW's objectivity and concrete-action focus suits outward, practical goals (a habit, a project, a performance target), while internal, meandering self-discovery issues "often work better with a more free-form approach" — which is exactly the gap the Coaching Funnel is built to fill.

## Why It Matters

A structured four-beat sequence turns "help me think about this" into a repeatable procedure that reliably produces a committed action, without the facilitator ever supplying the content. The transferable move is separating stages that get conflated in ordinary conversation — establishing facts before generating options, generating options before committing to one — and attaching a concrete confidence check to the final commitment so intentions that won't survive contact with reality get caught before the conversation ends, not after the plan has already failed.
