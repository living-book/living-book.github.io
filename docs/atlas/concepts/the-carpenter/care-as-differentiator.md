# Care as Differentiator

## Definition

Care is the third greatest success strategy. It means showing that you care about the quality of what you create and the wellbeing of the people you serve. Care is visible—in craftsmanship, attention to detail, follow-through, and presence. In a marketplace saturated with transactional relationships and indifference, genuine care becomes a competitive advantage that cannot be faked or manufactured.

## In the Book

The Carpenter carves "Care" onto the wooden heart and declares: "It's my favorite to talk about because when you care about the work you do and show people you care about them, you stand out in a world where most don't care. Caring leads to success!"

He illustrates: people flock to his burrito shop not because the food is unique, but because the people making it care enough to make it great. He can taste the difference. He can tell who cares and who is just collecting a paycheck. When people stop caring, he stops going—and so do others. Similarly, Michael's wife left a salon that became "too big too fast and didn't care enough." But the Carpenter's work is in high demand even though he returns no voicemails, because everyone can see the care embedded in every piece he builds.

The Carpenter emphasizes the breadth of care: "When you care, everyone matters and everything matters." Care is not selective; it extends to the work itself, the people you serve, the details others overlook.

## Why It Matters

This explains brand loyalty and word-of-mouth in ways that pure efficiency cannot. It also clarifies why "going bigger" often fails—scaling without maintaining genuine care hollows out the original value. For individuals, this is a permission to slow down and focus on doing fewer things with real care, rather than maximizing throughput. For organizations, it reframes the relationship with customers as one of stewardship, not extraction.
