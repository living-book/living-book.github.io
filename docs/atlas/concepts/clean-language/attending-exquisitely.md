# Attending Exquisitely

## Definition

Attending exquisitely means directing full, disciplined attention at exactly
what a person says (and does), noticing details they themselves may not be
conscious of, and reflecting their words back unchanged — "parrot-phrasing,"
not paraphrasing. The book treats this as a trainable skill distinct from
ordinary listening, because ordinary conversation is filtered through a wall
of unconscious assumptions and preoccupations that quietly substitutes the
listener's meaning for the speaker's.

## In the Book

Chapter 4 opens with a "how sure are you?" exercise: readers write confident
statements about an ambiguous picture, then are asked what would have to be
true for the picture to be as it is and the statement still false — and their
certainty collapses. It then works through real misunderstandings averted by
checking rather than assuming: a colleague who could have asked "What kind of
report?" before writing an unwanted twenty-page dossier, a chair who asked
"What kind of breaking point?" instead of assuming a meeting was collapsing,
and a party guest who asked "What kind of something to eat?" before panicking
about competitive cooking. The chapter gives twelve concrete listening tips
(soft-focus eyes, set your personal agenda aside, believe the words are
literally true for the speaker) and specifically warns that repeating back
someone's words in your own phrasing — however well-intentioned, as when a
chiropractor "translated" a patient's "not well-oiled" neck into "grinding" —
substitutes your model for theirs.

## Why It Matters

The gap between what someone says and what you think they mean is where most
miscommunication actually happens, and it is invisible from the inside — your
own paraphrase feels like an accurate summary even when it has silently
replaced their categories with yours. Training yourself to notice and hold
exact words, rather than your gloss on them, is a general discipline for any
situation where you must build an accurate model of another person's (or
system's) actual state before intervening: diagnosis, requirements-gathering,
negotiation, and conflict mediation all fail in the same way when this
discipline is skipped.
