# Metaphor Landscape

## Definition

People do not simply have isolated metaphors; their everyday, largely
unconscious metaphors about a topic (a feeling, a relationship, a life) knit
together into a single coherent "landscape" with its own internal logic,
elements, and spatial structure. Because thought and language are
fundamentally metaphorical (drawing on Lakoff and Johnson), David Grove
observed that when someone's metaphor changes, their real-life experience
changes with it — "it's as if they were their metaphors."

## In the Book

Chapter 3 grounds this in the Coins activity: readers arrange coins to
represent family members, and removing one coin carries real emotional weight
— evidence that metaphor triggers embodied, emotionally rich experience that
abstract language doesn't. The chapter distinguishes sensory, abstract, and
metaphoric information, and gives the example of eight-year-old Sam, who
described his listening problem as workmen's tools jamming machine cogs; once
a "boss" appeared in his imagined landscape to keep the workshop tidy, his
teacher (with no knowledge of the session) reported his classroom listening
had improved. Chapter 9, "Maturing Changes," extends the idea: once a change
appears in the landscape (the fog clearing to reveal a path, in one case), a
new metaphor is fragile "like a small seedling" and must be developed further
— explored for its effects on other symbols and over time — before it reliably
produces lasting real-world change.

## Why It Matters

Treating a person's spontaneous, unchosen figures of speech as a structured
system — rather than decorative flourishes — gives you a non-invasive way to
access how someone actually represents a problem to themselves, underneath
the words they'd use if asked to explain directly. It reframes "changing
someone's mind" as changing the shape of their internal representation, and
explains why the same advice or argument can transform one person's thinking
and bounce off another's: the landscape it needs to fit is different in each
case.
