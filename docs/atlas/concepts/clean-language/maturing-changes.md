# Maturing Changes

## Definition

When a shift occurs in someone's metaphor or thinking during a facilitated
conversation, stopping there is a mistake: the change is "like a small
seedling," easily undone by old triggers and other people's unchanged
expectations once the person returns to ordinary life. Maturing a change means
continuing to ask questions about the change itself, its effects on other
elements of the person's thinking, and its effects over time, until it is
robust enough to survive contact with the real world.

## In the Book

Chapter 9 gives the case of a client who announced "The fog has cleared! I can
see a clear path to my goal" — an apparent resolution — but further
questioning revealed the path was actually overgrown in places and lined with
distracting flowering shrubs she could wander off toward. Maturing this
change surfaced benches along the path where she could pause, take stock, and
plan; only then did she leave with an actionable real-life schedule. A longer
transcript ("It becomes a boat") shows a sales client's two conflicting inner
"parts" merging into a boat metaphor, then that metaphor being matured
question by question — what kind of boat, what happens to other symbols, what
happens next — until he reports feeling taller and able to look customers in
the eye. The book states experienced practitioners spend roughly a third of
total session time on maturing, warning explicitly against the temptation to
chase "what happens next" repeatedly without also checking effects on other
symbols, which produces a "flat" landscape.

## Why It Matters

An insight or breakthrough moment is not the same as a durable change — the
gap between the two is exactly where regression happens in coaching,
therapy, habit change, and organizational transformation alike. This concept
supplies a discipline for that gap: deliberately test a new state against
other parts of the system and against the passage of time before declaring it
done, rather than mistaking the first sign of movement for the destination.
