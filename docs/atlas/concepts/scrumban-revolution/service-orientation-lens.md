# Service Orientation Lens

## Definition

The service orientation lens is a perspective shift that reframes how an organization sees itself. Instead of viewing delivery as a set of functional silos (engineering, QA, operations, product) or a series of hand-offs among specialists, service orientation asks: What service do we deliver to our customers? What stakeholders depend on that service? What steps and decisions must flow through our organization for that service to reach the customer? This lens enables teams to recognize that workflow is how knowledge discovery and value creation actually happen, not how specialists are arranged.

## In the Book

Reddy emphasizes that service orientation is one of the Kanban Method's three core agendas (alongside sustainability and survivability) and that it represents a "radical change of thinking" for most organizations. The book illustrates that seeing services delivered to customers—rather than envisioning only a collection of functions and specializations—creates "a whole new level of understanding." Service orientation deepens knowledge discovery, improves lead times, enhances predictability, and minimizes variability. Critically, the book contrasts this with the common mistake in Lean value stream mapping: following the work through a series of specialists rather than identifying the dominant knowledge discovery activity at each stage. This latter approach leads to overly complicated visualizations and institutionalizes hand-offs. The book argues that Scrumban can be scaled across an organization in a service-oriented fashion by designing each service with a systems thinking approach.

## Why It Matters

This lens transforms how organizations identify bottlenecks and design improvements. Instead of optimizing for specialist utilization or process steps, service orientation drives decisions toward customer value and end-to-end predictability. It also reorients organizational sensing: a service-oriented structure naturally surfaces early when a customer's needs are shifting, because the entire value stream feels the change simultaneously rather than having it filtered through intermediary functions. This becomes a cybernetic mechanism for organizational responsiveness. Practically, it allows a single framework to coordinate across multiple teams and specializations without requiring reorganization—you overlay service orientation onto existing structure.
