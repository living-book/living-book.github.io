# Scrumban as an Evolutionary Framework

## Definition

Scrumban is fundamentally a framework of empowerment that supports evolutionary improvement at every stage of organizational learning. Rather than prescribing a single destination or replacing existing practices with a defined process, it provides complementary tools and capabilities that practitioners at different mastery levels (Shu: beginner following rules, Ha: intermediate discovering alternatives, Ri: advanced mastering adaptively) can deploy pragmatically based on their context. It succeeds by respecting current roles, responsibilities, and ways of working while catalyzing incremental improvements in response to discovered problems.

## In the Book

Reddy introduces Scrumban not as Corey Ladas's original "transition method from Scrum to continuous flow" but as a family of principles and practices that has evolved far beyond that single incarnation. The book distinguishes Scrumban as a framework that works at all three mastery stages differently: for Shu-level learners, it provides the discipline and structure needed to introduce Scrum ceremonies and Kanban practices in limited scope; for Ha-level practitioners, it exposes new tools that help teams navigate the challenges and experiments they encounter as they discover the limits of initial practices; for Ri-level masters, it is flexible enough to provide a robust process within which they can operate at hyper-performing levels while emphasizing systems thinking and discovery.

Critically, Reddy argues this pragmatic, bottom-line orientation—achieving improved service delivery of IT work and products to customers—is precisely what distinguishes Scrumban from "purist" frameworks. When strict adherence to Scrum fails to produce results, Scrumban does not blame the organization for inadequacy; instead, it provides mechanisms to discover and resolve the actual problems present, even if the resulting process looks unlike textbook Scrum or Kanban.

## Why It Matters

This concept lets teams and organizations adopt agile practices without requiring the disruptive, context-changing transformation that full Scrum adoption often demands. By starting with what exists and evolving incrementally, Scrumban reduces psychological resistance to change and allows organizations to build evidence and trust over time. Most importantly, it reframes "not doing it by the book" not as failure but as necessary adaptation—a distinction that makes the difference between sustainable improvement and abandoned initiatives. It is also the mechanism that allows a single framework to serve both teams just beginning their agile journey and mature organizations optimizing complex delivery at scale.
