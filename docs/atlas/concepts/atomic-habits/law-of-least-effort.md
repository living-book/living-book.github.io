# The Law of Least Effort

## Definition

The Law of Least Effort states that "when deciding between two similar options, people will naturally gravitate toward the option that requires the least amount of work." Clear treats this not as laziness to be overcome but as a rational, energy-conserving default of the brain that habit design should work with rather than against: reduce the friction (number of steps, physical distance, decisions required) standing between a person and a good habit, and increase the friction standing between them and a bad one.

## In the Book

Clear grounds the law in Jared Diamond's observation (from Guns, Germs, and Steel) that agriculture spread two to three times faster across the east-west landmass of Europe and Asia than along the north-south axis of the Americas and Africa, because farming along a shared latitude required no new crop domestication while farming north-south did — the historical spread of an entire technology was shaped by which direction required less adaptive effort. Clear uses this to argue that "our real motivation is to be lazy and to do what is convenient... this is a smart strategy, not a dumb one," then builds out the practical playbook: reduce the number of steps between you and a good habit (lay out your gym clothes the night before, pre-chop vegetables) and add steps between you and a bad one (unplug the TV after each use, remove an app from your phone's home screen).

## Why It Matters

This concept reframes "willpower failure" as a friction-engineering problem: the number of steps or amount of physical/cognitive effort required for an action predicts whether it happens, largely independent of how much someone claims to want it. It generalizes to any system trying to steer repeated choices — software UX (reduce clicks to the desired action, add friction to the risky one), supply chains (route selection follows the path of least resistance, not the optimal one), or policy design — wherever the honest lever is not "convince people to want X more" but "make X require less effort than the alternative."
