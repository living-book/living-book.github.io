# Liftoff

## Definition

A liftoff is an intentional meeting (or series of meetings) that brings all participants in a new team effort together to define shared intentions, approach, and plans while building the team. It generates the initial energy and clarity needed to overcome the natural inertia that emerges when new people come together around undefined roles and unclear purpose.

## In the Book

Larsen and Nies introduce liftoff as a response to a systematic pattern they observed across hundreds of retrospectives: when teams lack clarity and alignment at the start, problems emerge later. Miscommunication, unexamined assumptions, and misperceptions lead to poor decisions and team members working at cross-purposes.

The book describes a liftoff as occurring as early as possible—ideally before the team starts work on a new effort. When a team starts without clear purpose, alignment, or understanding of context, it can still hold a retroactive liftoff (a "restart") to get back on track. The authors note: "While how you start isn't the whole answer for a successful end, we've never experienced a work effort that started in disarray that was able to end well—at least not without a reset to clarify the core intention."

A real example is provided in the SAPHIR case study: a 14-person team drawn from four different European departments held a two-day kickoff workshop. During the first day, participants shared personal backgrounds, discussed the business case, explored work-package dependencies, and examined customer interface challenges. By the second day, after team members understood their interdependencies and team leaders recognized their input was valued, the team achieved cohesion that persisted through the six-month project—team members later cited the kickoff workshop as the turning point.

## Why It Matters

Liftoff creates a foundation that accelerates team development and performance. Without an intentional start, teams waste months discovering what could have been clarified upfront, and some fundamental misalignments never get addressed. By bringing the whole team and key stakeholders together in a focused, bounded event, liftoff converts abstract intentions into shared understanding. It establishes the container that holds the team's work, surfaces and legitimizes different perspectives, and creates shared language and identity—the conditions necessary for teams to move from forming to performing.
