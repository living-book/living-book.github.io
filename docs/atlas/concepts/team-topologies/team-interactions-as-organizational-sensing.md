# Team Interactions as Organizational Sensing

## Definition

Beyond delivering work, the points where teams interact with each other and with users can double as sensors: places where the organization detects that something is off and corrects course, rather than discovering the problem only after it has become large and expensive. This reframes front-line, user-facing roles (support, in particular) from a cost center to a deliberate feedback channel, borrowing the language of cybernetic control loops.

## Why It Matters

If the people closest to users or to a running system are treated only as executors of a fixed process, the organization loses its earliest and cheapest signal that something needs to change. Recasting front-line interaction as sensing — worth investing in as a skilled activity rather than minimizing as a cost — is a transferable idea anywhere an organization's edge (support, sales, field operations) touches reality before headquarters does.
