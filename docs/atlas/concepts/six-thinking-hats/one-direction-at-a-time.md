# One Direction at a Time

## Definition

The brain cannot be fully sensitized in two different directions simultaneously — searching hard for danger and searching hard for opportunity draw on incompatible configurations of attention, so trying to do both at once means doing neither well. Thorough thinking therefore has to be sequential: one mode run fully, then deliberately switched to another, rather than all modes blended into a single continuous judgment.

## In the Book

De Bono opens the book with an antelope grazing in Africa: a sound in the grass activates every neuronal cluster tuned to danger, so the moment a lion's shape emerges it is recognized instantly and the antelope escapes. That sensitization is precise and effective specifically because it points one way — a brain tuned to spot danger everywhere is not simultaneously the best brain for spotting a mate or a food source. He backs this with a design analogy: it is not possible to build a golf club that is simultaneously the best driving club and the best putting club. He later applies the same principle directly to the Black and Yellow Hats: it has been proposed that pros and cons should live under one hat called "judgement," but de Bono argues this fails in practice, because a thinker asked to weigh both at once ends up doing a diluted version of each — the black hat's specificity is what "relieves the thinker of the need to be fair and to see both sides... at the same time."

## Why It Matters

This is the mechanical justification for splitting any evaluation into separate, sequential passes rather than asking for a single balanced judgment in one breath: a reviewer told to find both strengths and flaws in the same sentence will underperform on both compared to two focused passes. It applies wherever a task requires switching between generative and critical stances, or optimism and risk-assessment, or exploration and evaluation — writing then editing, brainstorming then vetting, proposing then red-teaming. The claim is not that people should avoid one mode or favor another, but that doing them well requires doing them apart.
