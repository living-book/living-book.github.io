# Systems of Intelligence

## Definition

A system of intelligence is the "new machine": software built on massive processing power, huge volumes of data, and algorithms that learn, sitting on top of (not replacing) the older generation of systems of record such as ERP and CRM. It has the same basic stack as older software — app interface, process logic, database, infrastructure — but adds a learning core (machine intelligence) and continuous real-time data feeds, so it doesn't just record what happened, it decides what should happen next.

## In the Book

Chapter 4 lays out the "anatomy of a system of intelligence" in five layers: the app interface (the simple, FANG-quality front door users actually touch), the AI "innards" (digital process logic, machine intelligence, and a software ecosystem stitched together by APIs), the data flowing in from systems of record and instrumented "things," and the infrastructure underneath. The book contrasts this explicitly with systems of record: the same basic components, but reoriented around learning rather than transacting. Two worked examples anchor the idea. Uber is presented as automating the entire manual dispatch chain (customer calls in, agent informs dispatcher, dispatcher radios a driver) into one digitized process multiplied across millions of trips. Netflix is dissected layer by layer — its app, its recommendation-engine "machine learning," its process logic for infinite on-demand viewing — as a live example of a company whose entire business is, in effect, a system of intelligence wrapped around a media library.

## Why It Matters

Naming the layers of a system of intelligence gives a nontechnical leader a checklist for judging whether a "digital initiative" is real: does it have a genuinely simple interface, a learning core, live data feeds, and an ecosystem of connected services, or is it just an old system of record with a new coat of paint? That diagnostic travels well beyond software vendors — any organization retrofitting an existing process with data and automation is really asking whether it has built a system of record or a system of intelligence.
