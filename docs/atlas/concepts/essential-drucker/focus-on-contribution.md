# Focus on Contribution

## Definition

The effective person looks up from their own work and outward toward the goals of the whole organization, asking "What can I contribute that will significantly affect performance and results?" rather than focusing downward on effort, authority, and what the organization "owes" them. Whoever does this is, in Drucker's phrase, "in the most literal sense of the phrase, top management," regardless of title.

## In the Book

Chapter 14 contrasts two answers a management consultant gets when he asks senior executives what justifies their being on the payroll: most say "I run the accounting department" or cite headcount ("eight hundred and fifty people working under me"), while a few say "I am responsible for finding out what products the customer will want tomorrow." Drucker uses the case of a government science agency's publications director to sharpen the point: a new, more polished writer replaced a veteran non-writer director, and readership collapsed, because the old director had asked "what can I contribute?" and answered "interest young scientists in our work," while the new one optimized for professional polish nobody outside cared about — "the former director was writing for us; your new man writes at us." Drucker then names three areas every knowledge worker's contribution must cover: direct results, building and reaffirming values, and developing people for tomorrow — and notes that neglecting any one causes organizational decay, using the analogy of calories versus vitamins and minerals.

## Why It Matters

This distinguishes busyness and authority from contribution, giving a concrete diagnostic question — "what result outside my own function does this actually produce?" — usable in any role where effort is easy to observe but impact is not. It also warns against optimizing a proxy (polish, output volume, headcount) that correlates with contribution in some contexts but not others, a trap that recurs whenever an organization can measure activity more easily than the outcome activity is meant to serve.
