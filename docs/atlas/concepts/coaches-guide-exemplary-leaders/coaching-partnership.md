# Coaching Partnership Model

## Definition

Coaching partnership is a collaborative relationship structure where the coach and leader balance responsibility throughout the engagement, share skills and knowledge, maintain concurrence on decisions, and hold mutual accountability. It contrasts with expert-consultant or mentor-mentee models where the coach holds authority and prescribes solutions.

## In the Book

The book positions partnership as "the underlying foundation for your relationship," describing it as a bridge between the leader's world and the coach's expertise. Kouzes, Posner, and Biech argue that this arrangement—where leaders hold "the key to success"—increases consistent progress because leaders take ownership.

In practice, coaches model partnership through specific behaviors: being flexible with communication style, respecting diverse backgrounds, refraining from providing all ideas and solutions, and inspiring the leader during milestones. The book notes that early in a relationship, coaches may direct more, but must still position themselves as "someone who helps the leader find answers from within," not as the expert dispensing solutions.

The partnership is formalized through a "Coaching Partnership Agreement" that clarifies logistics (how often to meet, where), expected outcomes, documentation format, confidentiality, and how the partnership will address conflicts if they arise. Reaching agreement on these items—verbal or written—establishes the mutual contract that prevents mismatched expectations.

## Why It Matters

Partnership reframes coaching as a mutual endeavor rather than a remedial service, which shifts how leaders receive feedback and engage with development. Leaders who co-create their development plan and retain decision authority are more likely to implement changes and internalize learning. For organizations, partnership models reduce the perceived stigma of coaching (no longer punishment for underperformers) and increase engagement of high-potential leaders. This structure also distributes power in a way that mitigates coach dependency and builds leader agency—the coach is not the bottleneck for progress.
