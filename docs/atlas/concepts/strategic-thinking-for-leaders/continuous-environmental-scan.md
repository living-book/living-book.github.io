# Continuous Environmental Scanning

## Definition

Phase E of the ABCs is the ongoing scan of the outside environment for what might
help or hurt the plan before it happens — distinct from the other four phases
(vision, feedback, current state, actions) in that it never closes. Where A through
D form a cycle a team completes and revisits, E runs underneath the whole cycle at
all times. The book operationalizes it with the SKEPTIC checklist: Socio-demographic,
Competition/collaboration, Economics, Political, Technical, Industry/substitutes,
and Customer factors.

## In the Book

Tip #2, "Don't Step In It," frames the phase with a deliberately unglamorous image:
walking down the street distracted by your own success while either a hazard or "a
$100 bill on the sidewalk" sits ahead unnoticed. "You need to be continually
scanning the environment — what might happen in the future to you or your
organization... [b]y preparing for things that are coming at you, you will be ready
to avoid the obstacles or take advantage of lost currency on the ground." The tip
supplies the SKEPTIC acronym as the scan's checklist and closes with an instruction
that marks it as different in kind from the other phases: "IN EVERYTHING YOU DO,
SCAN YOUR ENVIRONMENT!" — not just during the annual planning offsite.

## Why It Matters

Most planning processes treat "environment" as a one-time input gathered at the
start (a market analysis slide) rather than a standing sensor that stays on while
the plan runs. Collapsing environmental awareness into a single upfront step means
a threat or opportunity that appears mid-execution has nowhere to enter the plan
until the next planning cycle. Making the scan continuous and structured across a
fixed set of categories (rather than whatever happens to catch someone's attention)
turns environmental awareness into a routine capability instead of a periodic
event, useful in any setting where the outside world changes faster than the
planning calendar does.
