# Mission Drift from the Job

## Definition

Most companies begin when a founder identifies an important job with no
satisfactory existing solution and builds something to solve it. As the
company scales, it typically redefines its own identity around the products
and services it sells — "quarter-inch drills" — rather than the job those
products exist to do — "quarter-inch holes." That inversion of identity is
gradual, usually invisible from inside, and is what makes a company
vulnerable to competitors who still see the underlying job clearly.

## In the Book

Chapter 8 opens by naming this pattern directly: "despite the best intentions
and a century of marketing wisdom, companies start to act as if their
business is defined by the products and services they sell... instead of the
jobs that they solve." It credits the quarter-inch-drill/quarter-inch-hole
formulation to Theodore Levitt (via his attribution to Leo McGinneva in *The
Marketing Imagination*), and the author notes he never once heard the
analogy during his MBA, his years at Boston Consulting Group, or his
doctoral studies — evidence, in his telling, of how easily something
"profound" becomes "obscure" inside organizations built around products. The
chapter's Questions for Leaders push the diagnostic directly onto the reader:
"How would your people characterize the fundamental business you are in?
Would they describe it in terms of solving an important job in their
customers' lives, or in terms of the products and services you offer?" This
drift is presented as the root cause that the Three Fallacies of Innovation
Data then reinforce and hide.

## Why It Matters

A company that forgets the job it was founded to solve becomes defenseless
against any competitor — however small or unconventional — who still sees
that job clearly, because the incumbent is now optimizing for its own product
line rather than for the customer's underlying problem. Naming the drift as
a distinct failure mode, separate from any single bad decision, makes it
something a leadership team can check for on a recurring basis rather than
discover only after a disruptor has already exploited it.
