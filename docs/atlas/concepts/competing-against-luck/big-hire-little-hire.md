# The Big Hire and the Little Hire

## Definition

The Big Hire is the moment a customer decides to buy or adopt a product; the
Little Hire is every subsequent moment the customer chooses to actually use
it instead of an alternative. Companies default to measuring the Big Hire
because it produces clean transactional data, but the book insists that "only
a consistent series of Little Hires can confirm" that a product has really
solved a customer's job — a purchase can happen for reasons (a promotion, a
gift, social pressure) that have nothing to do with the job the product ends
up doing, or failing to do, afterward.

## In the Book

Chapter 5's takeaways state the mechanism directly: "seemingly objective data
about customer behavior is often misleading, as it focuses exclusively on the
Big Hire (when the customer actually buys a product) and neglects the Little
Hire (when the customer actually uses it)." The chapter pairs this with a
second, related insight — that before a customer hires anything new, "you
have to understand what he'll need to fire in order to hire yours," because
adopting a new solution always displaces something else in the customer's
life, even if that something is doing nothing (a gap, a workaround, or
inaction itself). The "Questions for Leaders" that close the chapter push
readers to produce direct evidence of both hires: "Do you have evidence that
your customers make the Little Hire and the Big Hire?" and to trace the full
story from "a circumstance of struggle, to firing their current solution,
and ultimately hiring yours."

## Why It Matters

Sales and signup numbers are Big Hire metrics, and optimizing for them alone
can reward products that get bought once and then abandoned, ignored, or
used for the wrong reason — a gap that revenue dashboards will not show until
churn arrives much later. Separating the two hires forces a company to
instrument ongoing use, not just conversion, and to ask what the customer is
firing to make room for the new solution, which is often the harder question
to answer honestly.
