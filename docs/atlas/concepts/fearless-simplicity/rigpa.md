# Rigpa: Self-Knowing Awareness

## Definition

Rigpa is the self-knowing, self-luminous nature of mind that is simultaneously empty and aware. It is not something new to achieve but rather the natural ground already present within all beings. This awareness has three inseparable qualities: empty essence (free of any concrete entity), cognizant nature (the knowing quality), and unconfined capacity (the freedom to manifest in any way without restriction).

## In the Book

Tsoknyi Rinpoche teaches rigpa as the heart of Dzogchen practice. He explains that rigpa's three qualities—essence, nature, and capacity—are indivisible and beyond the categories of arising, dwelling, or ceasing. The empty essence provides freedom; the cognizant nature allows knowing to occur; and their unity creates unconfined capacity. Rinpoche illustrates this through the example of space in Times Square: just as space cannot be confined or made smaller, empty essence accommodates all phenomena. He emphasizes that recognizing rigpa is not about achieving something tangible but recognizing what is already present—"not something new that we must get our hands on." When the conceptual mind encounters this recognition, it cannot grasp the groundlessness and simply must surrender.

## Why It Matters

Rigpa is the framework that transforms how we relate to consciousness, emotion, and experience. Once recognized, it shifts the entire basis of practice from trying to achieve enlightenment to acknowledging the enlightened state already present. This recognition opens the possibility that no experience—pleasant or unpleasant—has the power to trap or harm us, because all experience occurs within the space of rigpa itself. This is what makes genuine fearlessness possible, not as suppression of fear but as fundamental confidence arising from understanding our basic nature.
