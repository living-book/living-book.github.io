# Rules: The Judge and Jury

## Definition

Rules are the specific criteria you've internalized about what must be true for you to feel successful, happy, loved, or confident. They are the internal judge-and-jury system that determines whether a value is being met and whether you give yourself permission to feel good. Most rules are unconscious and are rarely examined. They are formed through punishment and reward throughout childhood, and they shape behavior so powerfully that the same external outcome can feel like triumph or failure depending on the rule you're applying.

## In the Book

Robbins illustrates through the solar eclipse: thousands of people paid thousands of dollars to see a four-minute eclipse. When clouds obscured the view, some were devastated ("I spent $4,000 and got nothing"), some were curious ("This is still amazing"), and some found ways to enjoy it regardless. Same event, opposite emotional outcomes. Why? Different rules about what had to happen for the experience to count as "successful."

He describes a Fortune 500 executive earning $3.5 million annually who didn't feel successful because his rules required $3 million salary (bonuses didn't count), 8 percent body fat (he was at 9%), and never feeling frustrated with his five children. An impossible ruleset wired him for chronic failure. Contrast with a man whose rule was "All I have to do is wake up above ground"—he felt successful every day.

Robbins identifies the catastrophic pattern: most people have created dozens of ways to feel bad and only a handful of ways to feel good. If your rule for love is "My partner must read my mind and do exactly what I want without my asking," you'll feel unloved constantly. If your rule for success is "I must have zero failures," you'll avoid risk and feel like a fraud. If your rule for worth is "I must make everyone happy," you'll burn out.

The solution is not to lower standards. It's to redesign rules so they're achievable and aligned with reality. Instead of "In order to feel good, everything must go as planned," try "In order to feel good, I will find something to appreciate or learn from, no matter what happens."

## Why It Matters

Rules are the hidden lever controlling 80% of your emotional experience. You can hit every external goal and feel empty if your rules are misaligned. You can face setbacks and feel resourceful if your rules let you. Most people never examine their rules; they just wonder why they feel bad despite "having it all." Understanding and redesigning rules is more powerful than changing circumstances, because the same rules operate across situations. Fixing your rules for success makes you happier at work, at home, and in yourself—simultaneously. It's also the foundation for sustainable fulfillment: you can't rely on external conditions to stay the way you want them, but you can design rules that let you feel good anyway.
