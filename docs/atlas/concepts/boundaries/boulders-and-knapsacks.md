# Boulders and Knapsacks: Crisis Burdens vs. Daily Responsibilities

## Definition

Cloud and Townsend draw on the Greek distinction between two types of burdens. A "boulder" is an excess burden so heavy it would break your back—a true crisis that requires outside help to survive (illness, major trauma, financial catastrophe). A "knapsack" is the cargo of daily toil—the everyday responsibilities each person must carry alone (your feelings, attitudes, behaviors, daily decisions). Healthy boundaries require knowing which is which.

## In the Book

The authors introduce this distinction through Galatians 6:2 and 6:5. Verse 2 says "Carry each other's burdens," but verse 5 says "each one should carry his own load." These are not contradictory; they address different categories of problem.

A boulder cannot be borne alone. It will crush you. So when someone faces crisis—loss of a loved one, serious illness, financial collapse—others rightly step in to help. This is the sacrificial love of Christ modeled: doing for others what they cannot do for themselves in extreme circumstances.

But everyday responsibilities are knapsacks. You must deal with your own feelings, your own attitudes, your own behavioral patterns, your own disappointments. No one else can do your emotional work for you. These are possible to carry. The pain arises when people act as if their daily loads are boulders (refusing responsibility, demanding rescue) or treat genuine boulders as daily knapsacks (refusing help, isolating in crisis).

## Why It Matters

This distinction clarifies what healthy help looks like and exposes the mechanism of codependency. When someone constantly "rescues" another from daily responsibilities—covering for their irresponsibility, managing their emotions, making their decisions—they interrupt the natural consequences that would teach the person to grow. The rescuer doesn't help; they enable continued dysfunction. Conversely, refusing to acknowledge genuine crises as crises is cruelty disguised as boundaries. Understanding boulders and knapsacks means you can offer real help when it's needed without becoming responsible for what belongs to someone else alone.
