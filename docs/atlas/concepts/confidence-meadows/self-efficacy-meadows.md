# Self-Efficacy

## Definition

Self-efficacy is the strength of your belief in your ability to complete tasks successfully. It is task-related—you can have high self-efficacy driving a car but low self-efficacy running a business. Your beliefs about your abilities fundamentally shape how you think, feel, and behave, determining whether you persist through challenges or abandon goals, what objectives you set for yourself, and which difficulties feel merely difficult versus impossible.

## In the Book

Meadows distinguishes self-efficacy from self-esteem: efficacy is belief in your abilities, esteem is belief in your worth. A person can have high self-esteem (feeling valuable as a human) yet doubt their ability to perform a specific task. The book identifies four factors influencing self-efficacy in order of importance: mastery experiences, social modeling, social persuasion, and psychological responses.

Self-efficacy manifests behaviorally in two contrasting profiles. People with strong self-efficacy consider challenges things they can overcome, persevere when obstacles appear, take responsibility for failures (believing they control outcomes), put more effort into tasks, and commit deeply to goals. People with weak self-efficacy avoid challenges and rarely grow, believe difficult goals are beyond them, quickly lose confidence and give up, focus on surface details rather than principles, and attribute outcomes to external luck rather than their own actions. Meadows illustrates this through his own experience: low self-efficacy kept him avoiding uncomfortable situations until he deliberately challenged himself, building "small wins" that accumulated into confidence.

## Why It Matters

Self-efficacy acts as a self-fulfilling prophecy (the Galatea effect): your expectations largely determine your performance. Low self-efficacy leads to avoiding big goals, underinvesting effort ("why try if it won't work?"), and giving up when difficulty rises. The concept applies across domains—health decisions (whether you quit smoking, start exercising), career advancement (whether you apply for better jobs), and personal projects. Changing your belief in your capabilities unlocks agency: the same circumstances can feel impossible or merely difficult depending on your efficacy. This makes self-efficacy a hinge concept, shifting your relationship to challenge itself.
