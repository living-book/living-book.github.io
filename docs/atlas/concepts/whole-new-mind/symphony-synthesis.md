# Symphony (Synthesis Over Analysis)

## Definition

Pink defines Symphony as "the ability to put together the pieces. It is the capacity to synthesize rather than to analyze; to see relationships between seemingly unrelated fields; to detect broad patterns rather than to deliver specific answers; and to invent something new by combining elements nobody else thought to pair." He identifies three archetypal practitioners of this aptitude: the boundary crosser (who works fluently across disciplines), the inventor (who recombines existing elements into something new), and the metaphor maker (who understands one thing by explaining it in terms of another).

## In the Book

Chapter 6 is built around Pink's own five-day course in Betty Edwards's "Drawing on the Right Side of the Brain" method, taught by her son Brian Bomeisler. Pink's before-and-after self-portraits illustrate the mechanism directly: his first attempt draws "remembered symbols from childhood" (stylized, schematic lips) rather than what's actually in front of him; instruction improves the second attempt by training him to see relationships — the distance from eye-line to chin equals eye-line to top of head, the negative space in the FedEx logo's "Ex" that forms a hidden arrow. Bomeisler's repeated line, "drawing is largely about relationships," is offered as the same skill underlying Symphony generally: noticing how parts connect to form a whole, rather than analyzing any one part in isolation. Pink ties the aptitude's rising value to Automation and Asia stripping out routine analytic tasks, leaving "recognizing patterns, crossing boundaries to uncover hidden connections, and making bold leaps of imagination" as the residual, harder-to-replicate work.

## Why It Matters

Naming synthesis as a distinct, practiceable skill — with concrete sub-moves like noticing negative space or forcing an unfamiliar viewing angle to interrupt habitual categorization — gives a mechanism for "big picture thinking" beyond the usual vague exhortation. It's directly useful wherever specialization has been pushed to its analytic limit and the next gain comes from recombination: R&D, strategy, design, or any domain where the pieces already exist and the value is in the connection nobody else made.
