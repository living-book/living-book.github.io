# Expertise Is Narrow

## Definition

Expertise is a relative, socially judged, and domain-specific quantity — earned through sustained training and demonstrated ability relative to others — not a general trait that radiates from one field into neighboring ones. A person can be a genuine, credentialed expert in field A and have no more insight into field B than an educated layperson, even when the two fields sound related or the person's public stature makes the transfer feel intuitive.

## In the Book

Levitin's sharpest case is Dr. Roy Meadow, the pediatrician whose testimony helped convict Sally Clark of murdering her children: Meadow had genuine pediatric expertise but no training in medical statistics or epidemiology, yet the court treated his statistical claims as authoritative because he was a doctor. He pairs this with William Shockley, a Nobel physicist whose later racist pseudoscience gained undeserved traction because "he must know things others don't," and Gordon Shaw, a physicist with no behavioral-science training who originated the now-discredited "Mozart effect." Levitin generalizes with the mechanic analogy — a Toyota-certified mechanic is likely, on average, more expert on Toyotas than an independent, but that's a probabilistic edge, not a guarantee, and it says nothing about that mechanic's opinion on, say, tax law. He also notes actors like Sam Waterston, convincing as a TV district attorney, lending false credibility to real financial-services commercials.

## Why It Matters

This concept supplies a specific test for any appeal to authority: ask not just "is this person an expert?" but "an expert in exactly what domain, and is that the domain this claim belongs to?" It matters wherever credentialed people speak outside their lane — pundits, doctors testifying on statistics, scientists opining on policy, celebrities endorsing products — because audiences default to a halo effect where competence in one area is assumed to generalize. Naming the mechanism makes it possible to catch the substitution in real time, before a false transfer of authority does its damage.
