# The Age of Implementation

## Definition

After a rare foundational breakthrough (deep learning, in AI's case), the
economically decisive period is not further scientific discovery but the "nuts
and bolts" work of implementation — gathering data, tweaking formulas,
prototyping products, iterating business models. Popular attention gravitates to
speculative frontier questions (artificial general intelligence, superintelligence)
that are actually decades or centuries away, while the mundane engineering phase
that is already funded, already staffed, and already happening goes underrated.

## In the Book

Lee contrasts the utopian and dystopian camps — Ray Kurzweil's 2045 singularity,
Demis Hassabis's superintelligence-solves-everything vision, Elon Musk's
"summoning the demon" warnings, Nick Bostrom's control problem — with what he
argues is actually underway: the same deep learning technique from Geoffrey
Hinton's 2006 breakthrough being implemented across industry after industry, with
no comparable scientific leap in the twelve years since. He cites his own
miscalibration at Apple in the late 1980s, when he predicted mainstream speech
recognition within five years and was off by twenty, as a caution against
extrapolating research demonstrations into near-term timelines. His conclusion:
AGI remains many decades away, but the ordinary implementation of already-proven
deep learning is close enough to cause a real, near-term jobs and inequality
crisis regardless.

## Why It Matters

This separates two questions that get collapsed in public discourse about any
frontier technology: "will there be a bigger breakthrough?" and "what will the
current breakthrough actually do once fully deployed?" The second question is
answerable and often more consequential, because implementation compounds across
an entire economy while the next scientific leap is, by definition, unscheduled.
It is a general corrective for reading technological risk and opportunity off
the loudest speculative claims rather than off the deployment curve of what
already works.
