# Retrieval Practice

## Definition

The act of reconstructing knowledge from memory — through a quiz, a self-test,
or deliberate recall without looking — strengthens that memory far more than
re-exposure to the material (rereading, highlighting, reviewing notes). This
is the "testing effect": each successful retrieval reconsolidates the memory
trace, making it stronger and more accessible later. The effect is strongest
when retrieval takes real effort rather than being instant and easy.

## In the Book

Chapter 2, "To Learn, Retrieve," opens with neurosurgeon Mike Ebersold's
account of reflecting on a difficult surgery afterward — replaying what he
did and what he'd do differently — as a form of retrieval practice built into
professional life. The chapter's central evidence is a multi-year study at
Columbia Middle School in Illinois: researcher Pooja Agarwal and teacher
Patrice Bain introduced brief, no-stakes clicker quizzes covering one-third
of the sixth-grade social studies and eighth-grade science curriculum, while
another third was merely re-presented as review statements with no recall
required. On unit exams, quizzed material was scored a full letter grade
higher than unquizzed material, while material that was only reviewed (not
retrieved) showed no benefit over material that was never revisited at all.
The advantage persisted eight months later on end-of-year exams. The book
contrasts this against students' near-universal habit of highlighting and
rereading, shown in Chapter 1 to produce a feeling of mastery (fluency)
without durable learning.

## Why It Matters

It separates the *feeling* of learning from the *fact* of learning. Rereading
and review feel productive because they're easy and produce immediate,
visible familiarity; retrieval feels harder and slower because some
forgetting has already set in. Any system that optimizes for an easy, visible
proxy instead of the effortful mechanism that actually produces the outcome
will systematically under-invest in the thing that works — a pattern that
recurs well beyond studying, wherever the comfortable signal and the true
signal come apart.
