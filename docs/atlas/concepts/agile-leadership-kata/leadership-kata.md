# Leadership Kata

## Definition

Leadership Kata applies the martial-arts concept of kata—detailed, choreographed patterns of movements practiced solo or in pairs—to leadership development. Rather than haphazard leadership improvement, kata provides a structured, repeatable practice pattern that develops specific capabilities: Reverse Kata for problem-solving, A3 Kata for process management, Journaling Kata for envisioning, and coaching patterns for the facilitation domain.

## In the Book

The document defines Kata as a Japanese word describing detailed choreographed patterns practiced solo or in pairs, and Randori as free-style practice. Kata sits at the heart of continuous improvement, is about learning, and serves as a lovely metaphor for disciplined practice.

The book then maps four agile leadership competencies and their kata patterns: **Solve Problems** (using Reverse Kata: state the challenge, list assumptions, reverse each assumption, record differing viewpoints, ask how to accomplish each reversal), **Manage Process** (using A3 Kata and PDCA: background, current condition, goal/target condition, root cause analysis, countermeasures/experiments, confirmation/results, follow-up), **Envision** (through speaking, writing, reflection via 5 Whys and journals, including Journaling Kata: facts, feelings, findings), and **Coach/Facilitate** (through games and Remember the Future Kata: imagine future state, observe what's changed).

## Why It Matters

Leadership patterns without practice structure remain abstract. Kata provides the discipline that deliberate practice requires—a repeatable form that can be rehearsed until it becomes intuitive. This allows leaders to develop pattern recognition and judgment, not just conceptual knowledge. It also makes leadership practice observable and teachable, turning it from individual self-improvement into a communal capability.
