# Whose Problem Is It?

## Definition

Every problem belongs to someone, and misidentifying the owner leads to solving
the wrong person's problem — or solving it in a way they resent and won't sustain.
The book's rule for the common case where the owner also happens to be the
solver-in-residence is "DON'T SOLVE OTHER PEOPLE'S PROBLEMS WHEN THEY CAN SOLVE
THEM PERFECTLY WELL THEMSELVES." For the harder case — where the person able to
act doesn't personally feel the problem — the rule inverts: "IF A PERSON IS IN A
POSITION TO DO SOMETHING ABOUT A PROBLEM, BUT DOESN'T HAVE THE PROBLEM, THEN DO
SOMETHING SO HE DOES."

## In the Book

A cigar smoker in an otherwise nonsmoking classroom is fouling the air, and the
teacher — a "problem resolver" by trade — is tempted to mandate a fix. Instead he
lets the class run its own ad-hoc problem-solving session; within an hour the
smoker himself proposes giving up cigars for a rotating snack the whole class
shares, a solution that sticks because the group, not an authority, invented and
owns it. The chapter contrasts this with an absent-minded professor who, having
forgotten his wallet at a restaurant, always says "We have a problem," never "You
have a problem" or "I have a problem" — deliberately including the other party
in ownership. At a university campus starved for parking, the president who
issues the space-elimination policy has a reserved spot and never personally
experiences the problem; students respond by parking in his space so that he does,
escalating until faculty members declare, "It's MY problem" and start acting on it
directly, rather than waiting for someone with formal authority but no stake to
act.

## Why It Matters

Fixing a problem for people who could and should fix it themselves strips their
ownership and their motivation to sustain the fix, while fixing nothing for people
who lack the standing to act just perpetuates the problem. Explicitly asking
"whose problem is this?" — and, when the answer is someone insulated from the
consequences, deliberately engineering their exposure to it — turns a stalled
problem into one with an owner who is both able and motivated to resolve it.
