# The Law of Two Feet

## Definition

The one binding rule of Open Space: "If, during our time together, you find yourself in any situation where you are neither learning nor contributing, use your two feet and go to some more productive place." Owen also calls it the Law of Mobility for those who cannot literally walk. It relocates responsibility for the value of a conversation from whoever convened it onto every participant individually — nobody is obligated to stay somewhere that isn't working for them, and nobody else can fix that on their behalf.

## In the Book

Chapter VI presents the law as having four effects, all visible in Owen's descriptions of real events. First, it disciplines self-appointed experts and monologuists: "such people rapidly get a new and sobering message when half the room applies the Law of Two Feet and leaves." Second, it shifts the burden of a wasted hour off the group and onto the individual sitting through it — Owen contrasts this with the ordinary experience of "sitting politely, getting angrier and angrier, while our time is being wasted." Third and fourth, it produces two named behavioral types that Owen treats as legitimate contributors rather than problems: bumblebees, who flit constantly between sessions and "pollinate and cross-pollinate, lending richness and variety" across groups that would otherwise stay siloed; and butterflies, who attend no formal session at all, sit at the periphery, and — by doing essentially nothing — create pockets of stillness where an unplanned, often significant conversation can happen precisely because "nobody is looking for it."

## Why It Matters

Most group processes handle disengagement as a discipline problem — attendance is tracked, participation is mandated, people are told to sit still and pay attention. This law inverts that: it treats exit as a legitimate signal and a self-correcting mechanism rather than a failure to be prevented, and it converts what looks like unproductive wandering (the bumblebee, the butterfly) into a structural source of cross-pollination and unplanned insight. Any system that wants honest engagement rather than compliant attendance can use the same move — remove the obligation to stay, and let movement itself carry information about what is and isn't working.
