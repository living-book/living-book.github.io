# Co-Evolution of Problem and Solution

## Definition

Design is fundamentally different from problem-solving because designers do not search for a pre-existing goal or solution. Instead, problem and solution co-evolve. The solution concept is not "found" by analyzing the given problem, but actively created by the designer in a way that simultaneously redefines and refines the problem frame. The goal—what success looks like—is itself constructed through the design process.

## In the Book

Cross contrasts design with traditional problem-solving metaphors (Simon's ant, Jones's explorer) by noting that both assume an already-known goal that the problem-solver searches for. But designers work differently: "In design there is not an already-known goal; the designer creates the goal in creating a solution concept." 

The case studies demonstrate this coupling. Gordon Murray doesn't begin with a fixed goal for the racing car; instead, as he develops concepts through sketching and refinement, the goal simultaneously emerges. A sketch suggesting "one bit that looks good" leads him to drop other approaches and expand that direction, gradually defining what the car could be. With the city car, the concept of the single opening canopy emerged not from a pre-stated requirement but from exploring suspension trade-offs and manufacturing constraints—the solution concept (fewer openings = cheaper, lighter, more rigid) redefined the problem space.

Similarly, in team design tasks Cross observed, concepts like the "plastic tray" carrier didn't exist in the problem brief; they emerged when Kerry suggested "a bag" to hold loose straps, and John riffed on that with "maybe it's like a little vacuum-formed tray"—suddenly the concept existed and changed what the team understood the problem to be.

## Why It Matters

This challenges the sequential model of problem-definition-then-solution-finding common in engineering and planning. It explains why design briefs often seem underspecified or ambiguous—the incompleteness is intentional space for co-evolutionary exploration. Teams and organizations that try to fully specify the problem before solving risk freezing the solution space prematurely.
