# Specificity as the Manifestation Lever

## Definition

Stevens argues that how precisely a stated intention is worded, and how much felt alignment accompanies it, determines whether it produces any real-world change at all. Vague goals ("I want to get better," "improve my digestion") give the subconscious no target to act on and stay stuck; specific, first-person, emotionally-felt statements ("I choose...") are treated as directives the subconscious can execute quickly. He states the rule directly: "The more specific we are, the more instantaneous the manifestation. The more we feel, the more powerful the manifestation."

## In the Book

The "Role of Specificity" section in Chapter 2 walks through goal statements Stevens collected from health clients and class participants and diagnoses each as underspecified: "I want to get better" is flagged as "Want is lack, better is vague"; "To improve and not be sick all the time" is flagged for mixing vagueness ("improve") with negation ("not be," itself a death-metaphor in his framework) and for stating an "anti-goal" instead of a goal; "To have perfect health" is flagged with "what is perfect to you?" He uses a restaurant analogy — ordering "food" instead of a specific dish leaves the server (the subconscious) unable to deliver a satisfying result — and a marriage-proposal example, where a hedged "I don't know" or "I would like to" response is diagnosed as "indirect and weak." Chapter 3 formalizes this into two of the book's four "Basics of Conscious Language™": "Be first person, personal" and "Be specific," alongside "Speak what you choose" and "Be aligned in your feeling." Stevens's summary line: "Vagueness keeps our limitations in status quo."

## Why It Matters

This isolates specificity and ownership (first-person framing) as the two variables that convert a stated intention into an executable instruction, independent of whether the framework doing the executing is a subconscious mind, a team, or a piece of software — an underspecified request is a request that cannot be fulfilled or even checked for completion. It gives a concrete diagnostic (does this goal name who, what, how much, by when, in whose voice?) that transfers directly to requirements-writing, coaching, and any process where stated intent is supposed to cash out in action.
