# Wise Ignorance

## Definition

Wise ignorance is a deliberate mental state in which the coach completely suspends assumptions about where the coaching conversation will go, what the coachee should do, or what the "right" answer is. The coach trusts the coachee completely to answer in whatever way feels right for them, without needing to know in advance what that answer will be.

## In the Book

Dunbar develops this concept as central to the Clean Coach's way of being. The golden rule for working cleanly is "to assume absolutely nothing." The coach must not assume that the answer they are expecting and waiting to hear is the only or the best answer. By creating space inside their own head, coaches can "acquire a state of wise ignorance and be okay with not knowing what will happen next."

This state of mind can be developed with practice and the right attitude. It requires the coach to hold their own opinions and perspectives lightly, questioning them as thoroughly as they question the coachee. Dunbar illustrates this through the metaphor of "Clean Ears" (like Spock's from Star Trek)—technology that increases the range of information you can hear while simultaneously freezing the brain, slowing down your inferential thought tracks so you can take in what the other person says at face value "without attempting to make greater sense of it by seeking your own examples or experience."

She emphasizes adopting a stance of "wise ignorance"—if you think you understand, assume you are probably wrong and force yourself to check and test any understandings over and over again.

## Why It Matters

Wise ignorance paradoxically makes coaches more effective. When coaches release their need to know or predict the coachee's thinking, they create psychological safety for authentic exploration. The coachee senses they are not being steered and can think more freely. It also protects the coach from the cognitive biases that come from selective attention—noticing only information that confirms what they already think is true. This state of genuine open-mindedness allows coachees to access insights that would be impossible if filtered through the coach's preconceptions.
