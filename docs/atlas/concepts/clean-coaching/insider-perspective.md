# The Insider Perspective

## Definition

The insider perspective is the foundational belief that answers required for self-directed change come from within the person making the change, not from anyone else. The coachee is the expert on their own unique situation and worldview; the coach can never see the world as they do, even when believing they understand.

## In the Book

Dunbar establishes this principle in Chapter One as central to why Clean Coaching works differently from other coaching approaches. She argues that any attempt to help someone change from an outsider's perspective will fail, because as a coach "you cannot see the world as your coachee does, even when you think you understand—your view is distorted" by your own "map of the world."

All perspectives are inherently biased by personal experience and beliefs. The coach's view of what seems like an obvious solution for the coachee may be completely inappropriate from the coachee's unique, first-person position. However, the coachee is also biased by their own beliefs and experiences—"but as coaches we must work with these biases as they are the only reality our coachee knows."

When you start from the insider perspective—from where the coachee is now—shifts in perspective and ways of thinking emerge naturally as part of the change experience. Concrete behavioral changes often follow initial shifts in thought and idea. The book positions this against approaches that assume the coach should contribute expertise or direct the coachee toward "more viable options."

## Why It Matters

This principle reframes the coach's role entirely. Rather than importing solutions, the coach creates conditions for the coachee's own wisdom to surface. It explains why coaching that respects insider perspective often produces more durable change than coaching that imports expert advice. The concept also reveals why coachees often fail to adopt coach-suggested solutions: solutions imposed from outside never feel truly owned. Starting from insider perspective means trusting the coachee's capacity to find their own path.
