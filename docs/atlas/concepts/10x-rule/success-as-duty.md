# Success as Duty

## Definition

Success is not a luxury or an optional achievement—it's a moral obligation. Approaching it as a duty (rather than a wish) changes the energy and persistence you apply. When success is optional, it's easy to quit when resistance arrives. When it's a duty, quitting becomes a failure of responsibility. Cardone grounds this in family, community, and the future: you have a duty to create success for your children, spouse, organization, and legacy.

## In the Book

In Chapter 4, Cardone describes a turning point at age 25: "One of the greatest turning points in my life occurred when I stopped casually waiting for success and instead started to approach it as a duty, obligation, and responsibility. I literally began to see success as an ethical issue—a duty to my family, company, and future—rather than as something that may or may not happen to me."

He contrasts this with his education: "I spent 17 years getting a formal education that was to prepare me for the world—and not one course was on success." So despite schooling, books, and seminars, he had no sense of success as a necessary pursuit until he faced personal crisis—aimlessness, drug use, no direction at age 20-25. His survival instinct transformed his mindset: "Should I be happy with the love I have for my children and wife yesterday—or should I continue to create and pour it on in new amounts today and tomorrow?"

He contrasts obligation with the casual sentiment that "success is not everything." His response: "When someone in my seminars says this to me, I often respond by asking something along the lines of, 'Are you trying to diminish the importance of something you haven't been able to attain?'" Success enables you to provide, contribute, and leave a legacy. Failure to pursue it harms not just you but those who depend on you.

## Why It Matters

This shifts motivation from external (proving something, status) to internal and relational (duty to those who depend on you). It also legitimizes ambition: you're not being selfish by pursuing success; you're being responsible. This reframing can unlock sustained effort because duty is more durable than desire. Desire can fade when faced with setback; duty persists.
