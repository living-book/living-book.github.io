# Omnipresence Strategy

## Definition

The primary obstacle to success is not quality of product or even price, but obscurity—invisibility. Most great ideas never reach people because the entrepreneur assumes one or two marketing channels will suffice. The solution is omnipresence: operate as though your goal is to make your name, brand, product, and idea available everywhere, at all times, so that potential customers encounter it repeatedly and unavoidably until they know it.

## In the Book

Cardone opens Chapter 20 by noting that the most valued things on the planet—oxygen, water, electricity, Coca-Cola—are omnipresent. You don't have to search for them; you encounter them constantly. News networks achieve mindshare by broadcasting 24/7 in every format. His goal is to be so omnipresent in his field that "when they think sales training, they think of me."

He illustrates with his personal 18-month campaign after facing public attack: instead of retaliating, he followed his wife's advice—"the best revenge is massive success"—and focused entirely on omnipresence. He wrote three books, recorded 200+ videos, wrote 150 blogs and articles, did 700 radio interviews, and posted 2,000 times on social media. "My face, name, voice, articles, methodologies, and videos began showing up everywhere—many at the same time. People with whom I had never done business started saying to us, 'I see your name everywhere!'"

The principle extends beyond individual fame: every great company achieved omnipresence. Coca-Cola, McDonald's, Google, Starbucks appear on every corner. Your customers don't seek you out; they can't avoid you.

## Why It Matters

This challenges a common misconception about scale: that you can reach millions with modest effort. Omnipresence requires massive action across multiple channels simultaneously. But it's the clearest way to overcome the primary failure pattern: good product, zero awareness. If people don't know you exist, product quality is irrelevant. Omnipresence forces the necessary multiplication: one book doesn't create fame, but dozens of videos, articles, interviews, and social posts in rapid succession do. It's how you convert 10x effort into 10x awareness and opportunity.
