# Storage vs. Retrieval Strength

## Definition

Memory has two separate dimensions of strength. **Storage strength** measures how deeply something is learned and encoded—it builds steadily with studying and use, and crucially, *never decreases* once established. **Retrieval strength** measures how easily information comes to mind; it builds quickly with use but also fades quickly without reinforcement. Both dimensions increase when you successfully retrieve a memory, but retrieval strength fades passively over time, creating a gap that forces the brain to work harder on the next retrieval attempt—which paradoxically deepens learning more than easy, frequent recall.

## In the Book

The Bjorks developed this distinction as part of their "Forget to Learn" theory, grounded in the observation that forgetting isn't loss—it's inaccessibility. When you return to a fact whose retrieval strength has faded, the harder you have to work to find it, the greater the spike in both storage and retrieval strength afterward. Carey illustrates this with the analogy of a party where everyone you've ever met attends: storage strength is whether you recognize the person as familiar; retrieval strength is whether their name comes instantly to mind. A childhood friend (high storage, high retrieval) is obvious, but a first-grade teacher whose name you've forgotten (high storage, low retrieval) becomes much more memorable once reintroduced. Storage is permanent; retrieval is fickle. This separation explains why cramming doesn't last—it builds retrieval without storage—and why spaced practice, which allows retrieval strength to drop, produces such superior long-term retention.

## Why It Matters

This model reframes forgetting from failure to asset. It means old knowledge never truly vanishes; it becomes temporarily inaccessible, allowing room for new skills without interference. For learners, it justifies difficult, slow study—if something feels hard to remember, you're likely learning it more deeply than if it felt easy. For practical life, it predicts that switching languages or driving on the opposite side of the road becomes easier on return because storage strength remains high even after years away.
