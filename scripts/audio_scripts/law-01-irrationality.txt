You believe you are rational. That belief is the most dangerous thing about you. Robert Greene opens his laws of human nature here, because every other law depends on it. Your mind has two selves. A slow, deliberate, reasoning self. And an ancient emotional self that fires in milliseconds, then quietly dresses its reactions up as logic. You don't feel biased. You feel right. That's the trap. Ancient Athens had one man who understood this. Pericles. While rivals lurched from war fever to panic, he withdrew, consulted his own mind, and waited until emotion drained out of a decision. They called it Olympian distance. Athens thrived under it for decades, and collapsed into disaster soon after losing it. Here is the practice. When something stings you, an email, an insult, a setback, name the feeling, then wait twenty-four hours before responding. Not to suppress it, just to let the wave pass so you can see what's actually there. Reactions are instant. Rationality is deliberate. The gap between the two is where every bad decision of your life has lived. Master your emotional self, or it will quietly master you.
