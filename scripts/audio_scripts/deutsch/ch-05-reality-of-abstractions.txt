Are numbers real? Is knowledge real? Or are they, as the fashionable phrase goes, nothing but arrangements of atoms? Deutsch says abstractions are as real as anything, and he proves it with a machine made of dominoes. Imagine a vast array of dominoes wired up to compute whether a number is prime. You set it running, and one particular domino, the output, never falls. Why not? You could tell the microphysical story, this domino stood because that one stood because that one stood, all true, and it explains absolutely nothing. The real explanation is that the number is prime. A fact of pure mathematics, an abstraction, is doing genuine causal work in the best explanation of a physical event. So primality is real, by the only sensible criterion, if the best explanation needs it, it's real. This demolishes reductionism, the idea that only the lowest level, microphysics, truly causes anything. Explanation has autonomous levels, and causation runs through all of them. Here's the practice. Respect the level where the explanation lives. Some bugs are hardware, some are code, and some are incentive bugs, and fixing an incentive problem with a code patch is exactly the domino mistake. Same in organizations. It's all just individuals is reductionism. Team dynamics, institutional knowledge, morale, these are abstractions with real causal teeth, and your interventions have to hit the level where the cause actually lives.
