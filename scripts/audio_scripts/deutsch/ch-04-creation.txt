Where does new knowledge actually come from? Deutsch says there is exactly one process, and it built both the eye and the theory of the eye. Variation and selection. Conjecture and criticism. Neither evolution nor human thought copies knowledge from anywhere. Evolution doesn't learn from the environment. Minds don't derive ideas from the senses. Both create knowledge that wasn't there before, by generating variants and killing the failures. Consider Paley's famous watch on the heath, the finest wrong argument of its century. Surely such intricate design demands a designer? Darwin's answer was a population of replicators, varying blindly, filtered by survival, producing design with no designer. And Deutsch sharpens it with the modern gene's-eye view. What evolves is knowledge, in genes, for how to build the next generation. The deep point is a unification. Neo-Darwinism and the theory of human knowledge are the same theory, at different substrates. Replicators, variation, selection. Ideas, conjecture, criticism. Every notion that knowledge gets poured in from outside, by God, by the environment, by authority, is the same error in different costumes. Here's the practice. Your brainstorms and product bets are variation-selection engines, and starving either half kills them. No variation, one anointed idea polished forever, that's just Lamarckism. No selection, everything ships, nothing dies, no knowledge created. So ask honestly, what did we generate this quarter, and what did we actually kill.
