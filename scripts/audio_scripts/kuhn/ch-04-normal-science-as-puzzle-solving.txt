Here's Kuhn's most subversive reversal. During normal science, theories don't get tested. Scientists do. A normal research problem is a puzzle, like a crossword or a chess problem. The rules are fixed, and a solution is guaranteed to exist. That guarantee is what makes the work rational. Cavendish spending years on a torsion balance to weigh the earth never doubted Newton. The question was whether Cavendish was good enough. When a puzzle resists, the practitioner is blamed, not the paradigm. A poor carpenter blames his tools. It sounds like dogmatism, and Popper said exactly that. But Kuhn's point is deeper. Without that commitment, no one would ever push a framework hard enough to find its real limits. The puzzle discipline is what generates the precision that eventually makes deep anomalies visible. Doubt everything daily and you discover nothing, ever. Here's the practice. Before assigning or accepting a task, name what it is. A puzzle, where the frame is trusted and failure means escalate your skill? Or an open question, where the frame itself is in play? Most workplace misery comes from mislabeling one as the other. And notice your own reflex when things fail. Do you blame your skill or your framework? During normal work, skill first. Save the framework doubts. Their day comes.
