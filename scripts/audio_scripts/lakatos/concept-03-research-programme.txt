Here's the shift that made Lakatos famous. The unit of scientific achievement is not the theory. It's the research programme, a developing sequence of theories bound together over decades. And every programme has the same anatomy. A hard core of commitments its defenders refuse to give up. A protective belt of adjustable assumptions around it. And heuristics, rules telling researchers what to avoid and what to pursue next. Newtonian mechanics, Marxism, Freudianism, each is a programme, judged as a whole, over time. This is Kuhn's paradigm rebuilt as logic instead of sociology. What Kuhn called dogmatism, Lakatos calls the negative heuristic, a deliberate decision to protect the core. What Kuhn called normal puzzle-solving, Lakatos calls the positive heuristic, a planned agenda. Everything Kuhn described as psychology, Lakatos reconstructs as rational strategy. Take Newton in full dress. Hard core, the three laws plus gravitation. Belt, the assumptions about optics, planetary shapes, atmospheric refraction. Positive heuristic, the planned march from simple point-planets to spinning, bulging spheroids. Anomalies always hit the belt, never the core. Here's the practice. Model your own project as a programme. State the hard core in one sentence, the founding bet you won't renegotiate this year. Then notice, most so-called crises are just belt adjustments. A real pivot changes the core, and that starts a whole new programme.
