Popper said a single failed prediction kills a theory. Lakatos said that's a fantasy, and history proves it. His repair is elegant. A theory is never falsified by evidence alone. It's falsified only when a better theory arrives. And better has a precise meaning, in three parts. The rival predicts new facts the old theory forbids. It explains the old theory's successes. And some of those new predictions actually come true. Until that rival exists, refutation is just an unpaid bill. Two problems doom the naive view. First, theories never face nature alone. Every test drags along auxiliary assumptions, so you can always shift the blame. Second, every theory is born drowning in anomalies. If anomalies killed, nothing would survive infancy. Watch the maneuver in action. Uranus wobbled, violating Newton. Astronomers didn't abandon gravitation. They postulated an unseen planet, calculated where it must be, and found Neptune exactly there. Triumph. The same trick tried for Mercury, a planet they called Vulcan, failed forever. Yet Newton still wasn't dropped until Einstein offered a theory that fit Mercury and kept everything Newton got right. Here's the practice. Stop killing systems on bug reports. A grievance list is not a decision. Build the benchmarked rival that keeps the wins and fixes the losses, and let the comparison do the killing.
