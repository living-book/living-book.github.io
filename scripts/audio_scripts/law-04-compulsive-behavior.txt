People never do something only once. That's the heart of this law. Behavior isn't random, it flows from character, and character repeats. The colleague who betrayed a partner will betray you. The dazzling hire who left three teams in chaos will bring the chaos here. Not because they're evil, but because character is a pattern set deep, and under pressure everyone returns to their pattern. Howard Hughes is the case study. Brilliant image, famous aviator, endless capital. But underneath ran a compulsion to control everything and trust no one, and it wrecked project after project, the same collapse on repeat, for decades. Anyone who studied his track record could have predicted the next one. Almost nobody did, because his charm was louder than his history. Here's the practice. When you're about to trust someone with something that matters, a job, a partnership, a secret, ignore the interview and read the record. What actually happened in their last three chapters? Weigh actions, not explanations. And turn the lens on yourself. You have a pattern too. Find it, name it honestly, and you get the one power most people never claim, the ability to break your own loop instead of calling it fate.
