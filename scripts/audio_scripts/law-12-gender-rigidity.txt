You are performing a role that's using only half of you. Greene's law of gender rigidity says every one of us contains both masculine and feminine energies, assertion and empathy, analysis and intuition, firmness and flow. But culture hands us a script early. Boys harden into one register, girls into another, and by adulthood the unused half has gone numb. The cost is enormous. Rigid people are predictable, brittle, and blind in exactly the situations that need their missing range. Queen Elizabeth the First is the masterclass in refusing the script. In a world certain that a woman couldn't rule, she fused both energies into one style, iron judgment in negotiation, theatrical warmth with her public, patience where kings blustered, steel where courtiers expected softness. Rivals who read her as one thing were beaten by the other. Her wholeness was her advantage. Nobody could model her. Here's the practice. Find your default register, the energy you lead with under pressure, and this week deliberately deploy the other one, once. If you always push, try receiving and listening. If you always accommodate, hold one firm line. It will feel unnatural. That feeling is the numb half waking up.
