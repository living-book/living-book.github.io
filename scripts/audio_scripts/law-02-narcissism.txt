Everyone you know is a narcissist. Including you. That's not an insult, it's a spectrum. We all crave attention and validation. The question is what we do with that craving. Deep narcissists drown in it. Every conversation bends back to them, every criticism becomes an attack, every colleague becomes an audience. Joseph Stalin shows where that road ends. He demanded adoration, eliminated anyone who outshone him, and built a state on flattery. The result wasn't strength. It was paranoia so total he trusted no one and terrorized everyone. Contrast that with healthy narcissism, self-love secure enough to turn outward. That turn has a name. Empathy. Not the greeting-card kind, the muscle. Absorbing another person's mood, watching their body language, hearing what they don't say. Here's the practice. In your next conversation, ask three genuine questions before you say anything about yourself. Watch how the dynamic changes. People starve for attention. The person who gives it, honestly and fully, becomes magnetic. Greene's point is sharp. Your self-love is fuel. Burn it inward and it isolates you. Burn it outward and it becomes the rarest form of power, making others feel deeply seen.
