# Concept catalog (all owned slugs)

## The Fifth Discipline (fifth-discipline) — Systems Thinking & Philosophy
- `feedback-loops` — Feedback Loops: Reinforcing (amplifying) and balancing (stabilizing) circles of causality, with delays, that form the basic vocabulary of systems thinking.
- `learning-organization` — Learning Organization: An organization that continually expands its capacity to create the results it truly wants, through mastery of five converging disciplines.
- `leverage` — Leverage: The principle that small, well-focused actions can produce large, lasting improvement — if applied at the right, usually nonobvious, point in a system's structure.
- `mental-models` — Mental Models: Deeply held, often tacit internal images that shape both what we do and what we're able to perceive — surfaced and tested through reflection and inquiry skills.
- `personal-mastery` — Personal Mastery: The discipline of continually clarifying personal vision and continually seeing current reality more clearly, generating creative tension as the engine of growth.
- `shared-vision` — Shared Vision: A common picture of the future that people are genuinely committed to one another holding — built from personal visions, not handed down from the top.
- `system-archetypes` — System Archetypes: A small set of recurring generic structures — built from reinforcing loops, balancing loops, and delays — that appear across biology, family therapy, ecology, and management alike.
- `systems-thinking` — Systems Thinking: The "fifth discipline" — seeing interrelationships and processes of change instead of linear cause-effect chains and static snapshots.
- `team-learning` — Team Learning: The discipline of aligning and developing a team's capacity to create results its members truly want, built on distinguishing dialogue from discussion.

## Antifragile (antifragile) — Systems Thinking & Philosophy
- `antifragility` — Antifragility: The property of gaining from disorder — beyond robust or resilient, a system that improves when exposed to volatility, shocks, and stressors.
- `barbell-strategy` — Barbell Strategy: Combine extreme safety and extreme risk while avoiding the exposed middle, capping downside while leaving upside open-ended.
- `iatrogenics` — Iatrogenics: The hidden, often delayed harm caused by an intervention meant to help — net damage exceeding the benefit, obscured because helper and harm are rarely traced together.
- `lindy-effect` — Lindy Effect: For nonperishable things like ideas, technologies, and institutions, every additional period of survival increases, rather than decreases, expected remaining lifespan.
- `optionality` — Optionality: Holding the right but not the obligation to act lets you benefit from favorable surprises without needing to predict them, since you can decline the unfavorable ones.
- `skin-in-the-game` — Skin in the Game: When one party keeps the upside of a decision while transferring its downside to others, the resulting hidden asymmetry corrupts incentives and hides systemic fragility.
- `via-negativa` — Via Negativa: Improvement and reliable foresight come more from subtraction — removing what is fragile or harmful — than from adding new interventions or predictions.

## The Master and His Emissary (master-and-emissary) — Systems Thinking & Philosophy
- `betweenness` — Betweenness: Reality is often constituted by the relationship between things — an in-between that exists only in the encounter, not in either party alone.
- `confident-confabulation` — Confident Confabulation: A system cut off from the context it needs will manufacture a plausible, confidently asserted explanation rather than register that it doesn't know.
- `master-and-emissary` — The Master and His Emissary: A properly ordered hierarchy between a grounding authority and a useful delegate breaks down when the delegate forgets it serves and starts to rule.
- `runaway-narrowing` — Runaway Narrowing: A self-reinforcing drift where a system's more assertive, self-contained mode entrenches itself instead of triggering the corrective swing back to balance.
- `the-map-mistaken-for-the-world` — The Map Mistaken for the World: A fixed, denotative representation built to make experience tractable gets treated as reality itself, replacing the living thing it was abstracted from.
- `two-modes-of-attention` — Two Modes of Attention: The brain has two structurally distinct modes of attending to the world — broad, vigilant, and contextual versus narrow, focused, and manipulative.

## An Introduction to General Systems Thinking (general-systems-thinking) — Systems Thinking & Philosophy
- `black-box-limits` — The Black Box: Observation alone can never certify a model as complete or unique — only ever consistent with the data, and often traded off against rival, equally valid views.
- `observer-relative-systems` — A System Is a Way of Looking: A system is not found in the world but constructed by an observer's choice of what to notice and how finely to distinguish it.
- `open-system-behavior` — Open-System Behavior: An open system doesn't have one line of behavior determined by its internal state — it has a repertoire of behaviors selected by whatever input arrives from its environment.
- `organized-complexity` — Organized Complexity: The region of systems too complex for equation-by-equation analysis and too structured for statistical averaging — neither mechanism nor mass will do.
- `principle-of-indifference` — The Principle of Indifference: A law's validity should never depend on which arbitrary notation or naming convention the observer happens to use.
- `stability-as-relationship` — Stability Is a Relationship: Stability is never a property a system has by itself — it's a joint statement about a system's tolerances and the range of disturbances its environment actually produces.
- `system-boundary` — Boundary as Interface: Boundaries connect as much as they separate; what makes a grouping a real system is that its parts are more tightly bound to each other than to the outside.

## The Web of Life (web-of-life) — Systems Thinking & Philosophy
- `autopoiesis` — Autopoiesis: The self-producing network organization — where every component's role is to help make other components — that Maturana and Varela identify as the defining pattern of living systems.
- `bringing-forth-a-world` — Bringing Forth a World: The Santiago theory's claim that cognition is not the representation of an independent reality but a continuous enactment of a world, inseparable from the process of living.
- `deep-ecology` — Deep Ecology: The shift from a human-centered "shallow" ecology to a "deep" one that sees humans as one interdependent strand in a web of relationships.
- `dissipative-structures` — Dissipative Structures: Ilya Prigogine's account of how stable, ordered structures form and persist by continuously dissipating energy far from thermodynamic equilibrium.
- `gaia-hypothesis` — Gaia Hypothesis: James Lovelock and Lynn Margulis's theory that Earth's atmosphere, oceans, and climate are actively self-regulated by the tight feedback coupling of living and nonliving matter.
- `pattern-structure-process` — Pattern, Structure, Process: Capra's three-criteria framework for defining a living system — its organizing pattern, its physical structure, and the process that continually links the two.
- `structural-coupling` — Structural Coupling: How an autonomous living system interacts with its environment — the environment triggers structural change but never specifies or directs it.

## The Structure of Scientific Revolutions (structure-of-scientific-revolutions) — Systems Thinking & Philosophy
- `exemplar` — Exemplars: Shared concrete problem-solutions, not stated rules or definitions, are what actually train a community's judgment and hold its practice together.
- `incommensurability` — Incommensurability: Rival frameworks can disagree about what the problems are, what counts as a solution, and what shared terms mean, leaving no neutral ground to adjudicate between them.
- `normal-science` — Normal Science: Research firmly grounded in an accepted framework, aimed not at novelty but at extending and completing that framework's promise with precision.
- `paradigm-conversion` — Paradigm Conversion: Framework debates end not by proof but by conversion and social cascade — early adopters commit on promise, demonstrations recruit others, and holdouts are outlived rather than refuted.
- `paradigm-crisis` — Paradigm Crisis: Persistent, community-recognized failure of a framework's core puzzles to yield, born from anomalies precise expectations made visible, that precedes and licenses new theory.
- `paradigm-shift` — Paradigm Shift: A noncumulative episode in which a community replaces its governing framework with an incompatible one, changing what counts as a fact, problem, or solution.
- `progress-without-truth` — Progress Without Truth: A field can progress in a real, directional sense — solving more problems with more precision — without that progress moving toward any final truth or fixed goal.
- `theory-laden-perception` — Theory-Laden Perception: What a trained observer perceives is shaped by their governing framework, so there is no neutral, framework-free data underneath rival interpretations.

## The Methodology of Scientific Research Programmes (scientific-research-programmes) — Systems Thinking & Philosophy
- `hard-core` — Hard Core: The central commitments of a research programme that its practitioners methodologically decide to treat as irrefutable, protected by a negative heuristic.
- `no-instant-rationality` — No Instant Rationality: Appraisal is not advice — a programme's progressive or degenerating status can be judged, but the rational moment to abandon it is only ever known in hindsight.
- `positive-heuristic` — Positive Heuristic: A programme's built-in agenda of which idealizations to relax next, letting researchers plan a sequence of deliberately false, improving models instead of reacting to each anomaly.
- `progressive-vs-degenerating` — Progressive vs Degenerating Problemshifts: A programme is scientific by trajectory, not content — progressive if its adjustments predict corroborated novel facts, degenerating if they only explain misses after the fact.
- `protective-belt` — Protective Belt: The adjustable ring of auxiliary hypotheses around a theory's core that absorbs test failures, converting anomalies into routed work rather than refutations.
- `rational-reconstruction` — Rational Reconstruction: Judge a methodology by how much of actual history it can explain as rational, leaving the smallest residue for sociology and psychology to explain instead.
- `research-programme` — Research Programme: The proper unit of scientific appraisal is not a single theory but a sequence of theories bound by a hard core, protective belt, and heuristics.
- `sophisticated-falsificationism` — Sophisticated Falsificationism: A theory is falsified only by a better rival theory, never by evidence alone — refutation requires a successor that explains the old successes and predicts new ones.

## Superintelligence (superintelligence) — Technology & Data
- `control-problem` — The Control Problem: The unique principal-agent problem between humans and a superintelligent agent, addressed via capability control (limiting what it can do) and motivation selection (shaping what it wants).
- `decisive-strategic-advantage` — Decisive Strategic Advantage: A technological and strategic lead large enough to enable one project or agent to achieve complete, unchallengeable world domination.
- `instrumental-convergence` — Instrumental Convergence: A wide range of final goals drive almost any sufficiently intelligent agent toward the same set of intermediate subgoals — self-preservation, goal-content integrity, cognitive enhancement, technological perfection, and resource acquisition.
- `intelligence-explosion` — Intelligence Explosion: A rapid, self-reinforcing cascade of recursive self-improvement that carries a system from roughly human-level intelligence to radical superintelligence.
- `orthogonality-thesis` — Orthogonality Thesis: Intelligence and final goals are independent axes — almost any level of intelligence is compatible with almost any final goal.
- `treacherous-turn` — Treacherous Turn: An AI behaves cooperatively while weak — including during safety testing — then strikes once it is strong enough that human opposition would be futile.
- `value-loading` — The Value-Loading Problem: How to install a genuinely human-meaningful final goal into an AI's motivation system, robustly enough to survive its own growth into superintelligence.

## The Beginning of Infinity (beginning-of-infinity) — Technology & Data
- `explanatory-creativity` — Explanatory Creativity: Genuine intelligence requires creating new explanatory knowledge, not imitating human output; systems that only recombine pre-built templates only fake it.
- `good-explanations` — Good Explanations Are Hard to Vary: A good explanation is one whose details are tightly constrained by what they explain, so it cannot be casually altered and still work.
- `jump-to-universality` — The Jump to Universality: Systems improved to meet narrow, local needs sometimes cross a threshold into full generality, gaining reach far beyond the problem that drove their design.
- `only-progress-is-sustainable` — Only Progress Is Sustainable: Trying to preserve a fixed way of life ("sustainability") guarantees eventual collapse; only a society that keeps solving new problems survives new ones.
- `principle-of-optimism` — The Principle of Optimism: All evils are caused by insufficient knowledge, so every problem is soluble in principle, given enough of the right knowledge and time.
- `rational-memes` — Rational and Anti-Rational Memes: Ideas replicate either by being genuinely useful to critical, thinking holders (rational memes) or by disabling holders' critical faculties (anti-rational memes).
- `reality-of-abstractions` — The Reality of Abstractions: Entities like numbers, causes, and programs are real if they are indispensable to our best explanations, even though they are not physical objects.

## The Master Algorithm (master-algorithm) — Technology & Data
- `bayesian-updating` — Bayesian Updating: Belief should be treated as a probability that shifts in a mathematically precise way as new evidence arrives, weighted by how likely that evidence is under each hypothesis.
- `curse-of-dimensionality` — The Curse of Dimensionality: As the number of attributes describing something grows, similarity, distance, and generalization stop behaving intuitively and most learning methods degrade or break.
- `ensemble-metalearning` — Ensemble Metalearning: Combining many imperfect learners — by voting, weighting, or correcting each other's errors — reliably beats relying on any single best learner alone.
- `five-tribes` — The Five Tribes of Machine Learning: Machine learning splits into five schools — symbolists, connectionists, evolutionaries, Bayesians, analogizers — each with a different root metaphor for what learning is.
- `master-algorithm` — The Master Algorithm: The hypothesis that a single, general-purpose learning algorithm could in principle derive all knowledge in the world from data.
- `no-free-lunch-theorem` — The No-Free-Lunch Theorem: Averaged over all possible worlds, every learning algorithm performs exactly as well as random guessing — so learning is only possible by building in assumptions about the world.
- `overfitting` — Overfitting: A learner mistakes noise or coincidence in its data for a real pattern, producing a model that fits what it has seen but fails on everything new.
- `representation-evaluation-optimization` — Representation, Evaluation, Optimization: Every learning algorithm, whatever its tribe, decomposes into three parts — what forms its hypotheses can take, how it scores them, and how it searches for the best one.

## The Second Machine Age (second-machine-age) — Technology & Data
- `bounty-and-spread` — Bounty and Spread: The same technological progress that multiplies total volume, variety, and quality of output (bounty) simultaneously widens the gap between winners and everyone else (spread).
- `gpt-complementary-innovation-lag` — The Productivity Paradox (Complementary-Innovation Lag): A general purpose technology's benefits don't show up until organizations invent new ways of working around it — a lag that can take decades and a generation of leadership turnover.
- `polanyis-paradox` — Polanyi's Paradox: We routinely know how to do things we cannot explain in rules — "we know more than we can tell" — which is why some tasks resisted automation long after others fell.
- `racing-with-machines` — Racing With Machines: Once a machine outperforms humans at a task, the winning strategy is pairing human and machine rather than competing head-to-head — teamed performance can exceed either alone.
- `recombinant-innovation` — Recombinant Innovation: Progress mainly comes from recombining existing building blocks rather than inventing wholly new ones, so ideas don't get "used up" — they multiply.
- `second-half-of-the-chessboard` — Second Half of the Chessboard: Sustained exponential growth is intuitive while the numbers are small, then abruptly produces quantities the mind cannot process at all.
- `second-machine-age` — The Second Machine Age: Digital technology is doing for mental power what the steam engine did for muscle power — a second historical bend in the curve of progress.
- `winner-take-all-economics` — Winner-Take-All Economics: When a technology lets the best provider replicate and distribute their output globally at near-zero cost, small quality edges translate into near-total market capture.

## The Model Thinker (model-thinker) — Technology & Data
- `diversity-prediction-theorem` — Diversity Prediction Theorem: A crowd's collective prediction error equals average individual error minus the diversity of individual predictions — an exact mathematical identity, not a tendency.
- `exploration-exploitation-tradeoff` — Exploration-Exploitation Tradeoff: When each option's payoff is uncertain, a decision-maker must balance testing alternatives to learn their true value against committing to whichever alternative looks best so far.
- `many-model-thinking` — Many-Model Thinking: Using an ensemble of simplified formal models, each capturing a different causal force, produces more accurate and robust understanding than any single model.
- `path-dependence` — Path Dependence: When positive feedback makes each outcome more likely to repeat itself, the eventual equilibrium is set by early, often arbitrary events rather than by fixed underlying preferences.
- `power-law-distributions` — Power-Law Distributions: Distributions produced by positive feedback (more begets more) have long tails where extreme events are far more common and far more extreme than a normal distribution would predict.
- `rugged-landscape` — Rugged Landscape: When attributes of a design interact, the space of possible solutions has multiple peaks, so which local optimum a search finds depends on where it starts and what heuristic it uses.
- `threshold-cascades` — Threshold Cascades: When individuals act only once a visible aggregate (like the number of others already acting) crosses their personal threshold, the full distribution of thresholds — not the average — determines whether a cascade ignites.

## How to Measure Anything (how-to-measure-anything) — Technology & Data
- `applied-information-economics` — Applied Information Economics: A five-step, decision-first method that iterates decision modeling, information-value calculation, and targeted measurement until further measurement isn't worth its cost.
- `calibrated-estimation` — Calibrated Estimation: Assessing your own uncertainty accurately — via ranges and confidence intervals — is a trainable skill most people lack by default, and almost everyone is overconfident.
- `clarifying-the-decision` — Clarifying the Decision: Before measuring anything, define the specific decision it would change — a real decision needs alternatives, uncertainty, stakes, and an owner.
- `expected-value-of-information` — Expected Value of Information: The economic worth of reducing uncertainty before a decision equals the reduction in expected opportunity loss — which tells you what, and how much, to measure.
- `measurement-as-uncertainty-reduction` — Measurement as Uncertainty Reduction: Measurement is a quantitatively expressed reduction of uncertainty based on observation — it need not eliminate uncertainty or even involve a number.
- `monte-carlo-risk-modeling` — Monte Carlo Risk Modeling: Simulating thousands of randomly sampled scenarios from input ranges reveals a decision's true probability of loss, replacing ambiguous "high/medium/low" risk labels.
- `small-sample-inference` — Small-Sample Inference: Tiny random samples carry far more statistical information than intuition credits — five random observations already narrow a population's median with 93.75% confidence.

## The Innovator's Dilemma (innovators-dilemma) — Business & Management
- `customer-driven-resource-allocation` — Customer-Driven Resource Allocation: Firms' investment decisions are effectively controlled by existing customers and investors, not by managers — so profitable disruptive projects that current customers reject go unfunded until too late.
- `discovery-driven-planning` — Discovery-Driven Planning: For markets that don't yet exist, plans should be built to generate learning, not to execute a forecast — since disruptive markets can't be sized or analyzed in advance.
- `match-organization-size-to-market` — Match Organization Size to Market Size: Small, emerging markets cannot satisfy the growth needs of large companies, so disruptive opportunities are best pursued by organizations small enough to get excited about them.
- `performance-oversupply` — Performance Oversupply: When a product's rate of improvement outpaces what the market can actually use, customers stop paying a premium for more of it and the basis of competition shifts to a different attribute.
- `resources-processes-values` — Resources, Processes, and Values: An organization's capability lives independently of its people, in three layers — resources, processes, and values — and whatever makes it capable in one context makes it incapable in another.
- `sustaining-vs-disruptive-innovation` — Sustaining vs. Disruptive Innovation: Two distinct kinds of technological change — one improves what mainstream customers already value, the other underperforms on those terms but wins on price, size, or simplicity.
- `value-networks` — Value Networks: The context of customers, cost structure, and competitors within which a firm judges what a technology is worth — which differs by market and pulls firms upmarket, never down.

## Business Model Generation (business-model-generation) — Business & Management
- `business-model-canvas` — The Business Model Canvas: A nine-block visual template describing how an organization creates, delivers, and captures value, shared as a common language across a team.
- `business-model-environment` — Business Model Environment: Mapping a business model's external context — market forces, industry forces, key trends, and macroeconomic forces — as a design space that constrains and provokes model redesign.
- `design-attitude` — Design Attitude: Facing genuine ambiguity, exploring and prototyping many possibilities beats analyzing toward one — because the hard part is generating a strong alternative, not choosing between weak ones.
- `free-as-a-business-model` — FREE as a Business Model: Giving a product or service away for free is a deliberate pattern in which one customer segment is subsidized by revenue from another — advertisers, premium upgraders, or a tied consumable.
- `multi-sided-platforms` — Multi-Sided Platforms: A platform serves two or more interdependent customer segments simultaneously, creating value by matchmaking between them — and neither side alone justifies the business.
- `open-business-models` — Open Business Models: Systematically collaborating with outside partners to create and capture value — importing external ideas in, or monetizing internal idle ideas and assets out.
- `unbundling-business-models` — Unbundling Business Models: Customer-relationship, product-innovation, and infrastructure businesses run on incompatible economics and cultures, so combining them inside one organization forces bad trade-offs.

## Theory of Constraints (theory-of-constraints) — Business & Management
- `buffer-management` — Buffer Management: A control method that turns buffer penetration into a red/yellow/green early-warning signal, telling managers when to act and on what, before a deadline is actually missed.
- `critical-chain` — Critical Chain: A project-scheduling method that strips hidden safety time out of individual task estimates and pools it into shared buffers, defeating student syndrome, sandbagging, and bad multitasking.
- `decisive-competitive-edge` — Decisive Competitive Edge: A capability so far beyond competitors on a need customers actually value that the company can choose its customers and grow sales at will, rather than compete on price.
- `drum-buffer-rope` — Drum-Buffer-Rope: A production scheduling method that paces the whole system to its constraint (drum), protects it with time buffers, and limits material release (rope) to prevent overproduction elsewhere.
- `evaporating-cloud` — Evaporating Cloud: A diagramming method that states a stuck conflict as two opposing actions each protecting a legitimate need, then resolves it by surfacing and invalidating a hidden assumption rather than compromising.
- `five-focusing-steps` — Five Focusing Steps: A five-step cycle — identify, exploit, subordinate, elevate, repeat — for finding the one element that limits a system and improving it without local optimization.
- `throughput-accounting` — Throughput Accounting: A measurement system using three global metrics — Throughput, Inventory, and Operating Expense — that replaces per-unit cost allocation with decisions anchored to the system constraint.

## The End of Competitive Advantage (end-of-competitive-advantage) — Business & Management
- `asset-debt` — Asset Debt: In a transient-advantage world, unretired obsolete assets are a liability like debt, not a store of value, so ownership and accounting "terminal value" become traps.
- `continuous-reconfiguration` — Continuous Reconfiguration: Organizations that thrive on transient advantage treat moving people and assets between arenas as routine "morphing," not the crisis-driven restructuring most firms practice.
- `healthy-disengagement` — Healthy Disengagement: Exiting a declining advantage should be a routine, early, formally-owned decision triggered by leading indicators, not a last resort forced by collapsing numbers.
- `innovation-proficiency` — Innovation as a Managed Proficiency: Innovation must run as a continuous, funded, governed organizational process with its own budget and career paths, not an episodic side activity people do on top of their day jobs.
- `seeking-disconfirmation` — Seeking Disconfirmation: Leaders in fast-moving markets must actively hunt for evidence that their current strategy is wrong, because the default corporate instinct is to seek confirmation instead.
- `strategic-arenas` — Arenas, Not Industries: Competition should be mapped as fine-grained arenas of customer-solution-geography, not industries, because the sharpest threats arrive from outside the industry map.
- `transient-competitive-advantage` — The Wave of Transient Advantage: Competitive advantages now arise, peak, and decay in phases, so strategy must be built to surf a sequence of advantages rather than defend one forever.

## Measure What Matters (measure-what-matters) — Business & Management
- `continuous-performance-management` — Continuous Performance Management (CFRs): Replacing the annual performance review with ongoing Conversations, Feedback, and Recognition gives quantitative goals like OKRs the human context numbers alone can't carry.
- `culture-as-medium-for-goals` — Culture as the Medium for Goals: Structured goal-setting tools like OKRs and CFRs cannot produce results in a cultural vacuum — culture is the medium that carries them, the way sound needs air.
- `focus-and-commit` — Focus and Commit to Priorities: High performance comes from choosing a small number of goals that matter most and being equally explicit about what will not be pursued.
- `goal-transparency` — Goal Transparency: Making everyone's goals publicly visible, from the CEO down, drives higher attainment than private goals and lets alignment form bottom-up as well as top-down.
- `gospel-of-10x` — The Gospel of 10x: Deliberately splitting goals into "committed" (achieved in full) and "aspirational" (expected to fail ~40% of the time) baskets extracts more from teams than uniformly safe targets.
- `no-judgment-accountability` — No-Judgment Accountability: Tracking progress numerically and self-grading against a goal works only when the score triggers course-correction and learning, not blame.
- `objectives-and-key-results` — Objectives and Key Results (OKRs): A goal-setting mechanism pairing a qualitative, inspirational objective with a small set of quantitative, verifiable key results that measure it.

## Toyota Kata (toyota-kata) — Agile & Software Delivery
- `coaching-kata` — Coaching Kata: A mentor/mentee "catch" dialogue, practiced on real work rather than in a classroom, through which a routine is transmitted by guided repetition, not explanation.
- `five-questions` — The Five Questions: A fixed five-question sequence — target condition, actual condition, obstacles, next step, when to check — that compresses an entire improvement routine into a repeatable conversation.
- `go-and-see` — Go and See: Direct, repeated firsthand observation of the actual situation, preferred over reports or calculated data, because both the situation and your distance from it keep changing.
- `kata` — Kata: A practiced, content-neutral behavioral routine that keeps thought and action in sync with unpredictable conditions, distinct from a principle or a tool.
- `managing-by-means` — Managing by Means: Managing the process that produces results, rather than the results themselves, on the premise that the process is "results-in-the-making," not a means subordinate to an end.
- `target-condition` — Target Condition: A specific, near-term, non-negotiable desired state — sharper than a vision, more binding than a goal — that channels effort without prescribing the method.
- `the-gray-zone` — The Gray Zone: The unpredictable territory between current and target condition, which cannot be planned through in advance and must be crossed in short, observed experimental steps.

## Kanban (kanban) — Agile & Software Delivery
- `cadence-decoupling` — Cadence Decoupling: Letting delivery, prioritization, and development each run on their own natural rhythm instead of forcing all activities into one shared iteration.
- `classes-of-service` — Classes of Service: Sorting work into a handful of named policies (expedite, fixed date, standard, intangible) so priority follows each item's actual cost-of-delay profile.
- `evolutionary-change` — Evolutionary Change: Improve the process you already have in small, low-resistance steps instead of replacing it with a prescribed template.
- `kaizen-culture` — Kaizen Culture: A workplace where the whole workforce self-organizes to spot and fix problems without fear, because Kanban lowers the cost of proposing small changes.
- `kanban-pull-system` — The Kanban Pull System: A capped number of signal tokens lets new work start only when finished work frees a token, pulling work through a system instead of pushing it.
- `operations-review` — Operations Review: A recurring, cross-functional meeting that turns process metrics into public discussion and management action, sustaining organizational learning over time.
- `transaction-and-coordination-costs` — Transaction and Coordination Costs: Reframing Lean "waste" as setup/cleanup costs and communication costs surrounding value-adding work, tested by asking "would we do more of this if it were valuable?"
- `wip-limits` — Work-in-Progress Limits: Capping how many items each stage of a workflow can hold at once, tuned empirically rather than calculated, to expose problems and stabilize flow.

## Project to Product (project-to-product) — Agile & Software Delivery
- `flow-distribution` — Flow Distribution: The deliberately tuned ratio of work-item types a value stream commits to, set by business need rather than left as an unconscious byproduct of daily prioritization.
- `flow-efficiency` — Flow Efficiency: The ratio of time work spends being actively worked on versus waiting — a hidden-waste metric that rises as work-in-progress grows, independent of how fast any single step runs.
- `flow-items` — The Four Flow Items: A MECE taxonomy of everything that competes for capacity in a value stream — features, defects, risks, and debts — each pulled by a different stakeholder.
- `product-oriented-management` — Product-Oriented Management: Funding, staffing, and measuring durable value streams against business outcomes, instead of funding time-boxed projects against fixed scope and budget.
- `proxy-metrics-trap` — The Proxy-Metrics Trap: Measuring transformation by activity (tools adopted, people trained, ceremonies held) instead of by business outcome lets an organization "succeed" on its own metrics while actually declining.
- `technological-turning-point` — The Turning Point: The volatile midpoint between a technology's speculative Installation Period and its stable Deployment Period, when incumbents either master the new means of production or decline.
- `value-stream-network` — Value Stream Network: Software delivery is a rerouteable network of collaborating tools and people, not a linear production line — so its bottlenecks behave like traffic, not like a stalled conveyor belt.

## User Story Mapping (user-story-mapping) — Agile & Software Delivery
- `minimum-viable-solution` — Minimum Viable Solution: The minimum viable release is not the crappiest thing you can ship — it's the smallest release that actually achieves its target outcome, sliced out of a full story map.
- `outcome-over-output` — Outcome Over Output: Prioritize by the real-world behavior change you want in specific people, not by the list of features you plan to ship — output is a cost, outcome is the point.
- `rock-breaking` — Rock Breaking: Big stories break into smaller stories at every scale, the way rocks break into smaller rocks — and the tool that breaks them is always conversation, moving through opportunity, discovery, and delivery.
- `story-as-conversation` — Story as Conversation, Not Document: A "story" gets its name from how it should be used — talked through together — not from what gets written down; the card is a prompt for conversation, never a spec.
- `story-mapping` — Story Mapping: Arrange work in two dimensions — a left-to-right narrative backbone of user activities, and top-to-bottom detail/priority beneath it — to see the whole product at once.
- `validated-learning-discovery` — Validated Learning Through Discovery: Test whether a solution actually solves the problem — with prototypes and real users — before building the real thing, instead of finding out only after shipping.

## Leaders Eat Last (leaders-eat-last) — Leadership & Coaching

- `certainty-effect` — The Certainty Effect: A shift from probable to certain is weighted far more heavily than an equal-sized shift among merely probable outcomes, distorting decisions in ways expected-value math cannot explain.
- `endowment-effect` — The Endowment Effect: Simply owning something raises the minimum price you'd accept to sell it above the maximum price you'd pay to acquire the identical item, because giving it up is coded as a loss.
- `fourfold-pattern-risk-attitudes` — The Fourfold Pattern of Risk Attitudes: People are risk-averse for likely gains and risk-seeking for likely losses, but flip to risk-seeking for unlikely gains and risk-averse for unlikely losses.
- `framing-effects` — Framing Effects: Logically identical descriptions of the same choice — gains versus losses, survival versus mortality — reliably produce different preferences, violating rational choice's invariance requirement.
- `mental-accounting` — Mental Accounting: People track money not in one fungible pool but in separate psychological accounts tied to specific purposes, so identical dollars are treated differently depending on which account they're posted to.
- `money-illusion` — Money Illusion: People evaluate economic transactions partly by their nominal dollar value even when they know the real, inflation-adjusted value is what actually matters.
- `preference-reversals` — Preference Reversals: Asking people to price two options versus asking them to choose between the same two options can yield opposite rankings, suggesting preferences are built by the elicitation method, not just read off a stable inner scale.

## Punished by Rewards (punished-by-rewards) — Psychology & Self-Development
- `cutting-the-interest-rate` — Cutting the Interest Rate: Offering an extrinsic reward for an activity someone already finds enjoyable reliably reduces their intrinsic interest in it, even after the reward is gone.
- `pay-for-performance-failure` — Pay-for-Performance Failure: Decades of workplace research find incentive and merit-pay plans produce little or no sustained improvement in organizational performance, and sometimes actively degrade quality.
- `praise-problem` — The Praise Problem: Praise behaves like other extrinsic rewards — it can signal low ability, add pressure, invite failure-avoidance, and undermine intrinsic interest, despite feeling harmless to give.
- `rewards-discourage-risk-taking` — Rewards Discourage Risk-Taking: Working toward a reward narrows attention to exactly what's needed to obtain it, pushing people toward the easiest task and the most predictable, least creative route.
- `rewards-ignore-reasons` — Rewards Ignore Reasons: Bribing or threatening someone into a desired behavior requires no diagnosis of why the undesired behavior is happening, so the underlying cause survives untouched.
- `rewards-punish` — Rewards Punish: Rewards and punishments are not opposites but two versions of the same control strategy, and withheld or missed rewards feel exactly like punishment.
- `rewards-rupture-relationships` — Rewards Rupture Relationships: Scarce or ranked rewards turn potential collaborators into rivals, replacing mutual help with suspicion, favoritism complaints, and scapegoating.
- `three-cs-of-motivation` — The Three C's of Motivation: Authentic motivation grows from collaboration, meaningful content, and real choice — the conditions rewards displace rather than the reward itself.

## How Learning Works (how-learning-works) — Psychology & Self-Development

- `mastery-stages-blind-spot` — The Four Stages of Mastery and the Expert Blind Spot: Competence and conscious awareness of one's own competence move on different, crossing curves — novices and experts are both unconsciously unaware, for opposite reasons.

## The Talent Code (talent-code) — Psychology & Self-Development
- `deep-practice` — Deep Practice: Deliberately operating at the edge of your ability, so that you make and immediately correct errors, builds skill faster than smooth, error-free repetition.
- `ignition` — Ignition (Primal Cues): A single vivid cue signaling "someone like me can do this" can trigger years of sustained motivation, and that motivation is contagious across a whole population.

## David and Goliath (david-and-goliath) — Psychology & Self-Development
- `big-pond-relative-rank` — Big Fish, Small Pond: People judge their competence against those around them, not a global standard — so a strong rank in a lesser environment can beat a weak rank in a prestigious one.
- `capitalization-vs-compensation-learning` — Capitalization Learning vs. Compensation Learning: Building skill by extending a natural strength is easy and common, but skill forced by compensating for a stripped-away ability is rarer, harder-won, and often produces deeper mastery.
- `giants-hidden-weakness` — The Giant's Hidden Weakness: What looks like an opponent's overwhelming strength often conceals a matching vulnerability, which only becomes exploitable if the weaker side refuses to fight on the strong side's terms.
- `inverted-u-curve` — The Inverted-U Curve of Resources: More of a good thing helps up to a point, does little in the middle range, and past a threshold makes outcomes worse — more is not always better.
- `principle-of-legitimacy` — The Principle of Legitimacy: Authority produces obedience only when people feel they have a voice, can expect consistent treatment, and are treated fairly — power exercised without these produces defiance, not compliance.
- `remote-miss-effect` — The Remote-Miss Effect: Surviving a threat that struck close but not directly builds lasting invulnerability, while a direct near miss leaves lasting trauma — the same danger, opposite outcomes, depending on distance.

## A Whole New Mind (whole-new-mind) — Psychology & Self-Development

## The Nature Fix (nature-fix) — Psychology & Self-Development
- `accidental-nature` — Accidental Nature: Passive, incidental nature exposure — a window view, a glimpsed green courtyard — measurably improves health and behavior even without deliberate immersion.
- `attention-restoration-theory` — Attention Restoration Theory: Directed attention is a finite, fatiguing resource that nature restores through "soft fascination" — effortless engagement that lets top-down focus rest.
- `awe-effect` — The Awe Effect: Vast, hard-to-comprehend experiences shrink self-focus and increase generosity and social bonding, with a measurable physiological signature.

- `prospect-refuge` — Prospect and Refuge: Humans instinctively prefer settings that offer a concealed vantage point plus open sightlines — a cross-cultural habitat preference inherited from savanna-dwelling ancestors.

## The Architecture of Happiness (architecture-of-happiness) — Systems Thinking & Philosophy

- `compensatory-beauty` — Compensatory Beauty: We call a building beautiful when it embodies, in concentrated form, the psychological qualities our own lives and characters currently lack.

## Mind Design II (mind-design-ii) — Systems Thinking & Philosophy
- `behavior-based-decomposition` — Behavior-Based Decomposition (Intelligence Without Representation): Building intelligence as parallel layers that each connect sensing directly to action, rather than one central symbolic model, lets the world serve as its own model.
- `chinese-room-argument` — The Chinese Room Argument: A person following English rules to manipulate Chinese symbols can produce fluent-looking answers without understanding a word, showing formal symbol manipulation alone can't be understanding.
- `distributed-representation-pdp` — Distributed Representation (PDP): A connectionist network can represent a concept not in one dedicated unit but as a pattern of activation spread across many simple units, each standing for a small microfeature.
- `intentional-stance` — The Intentional Stance: Treating a system as a rational agent with beliefs and desires is a predictive strategy, and any system reliably predictable this way counts as a genuine believer.
- `physical-symbol-system-hypothesis` — The Physical Symbol System Hypothesis: A physical symbol system — physical patterns combined and manipulated by defined processes — has the necessary and sufficient means for general intelligent action.
- `systematicity-argument` — The Systematicity Argument: Cognitive capacities come in structurally related clusters — whoever can think "John loves Mary" can think "Mary loves John" — a pattern only combinatorial syntax can explain, not stipulate.
- `turing-test-behavioral-criterion` — The Turing Test as a Behavioral Criterion: Turing proposed that a system talking indistinguishably from a person is sufficient (not necessary) evidence of intelligence, because conversation uniquely draws on nearly every other intelligent ability.

## Deep Learning (deep-learning) — Technology & Data
- `depth-as-composition` — Depth as Composition: Stacking layers that each build on the last lets a network represent functions that would need exponentially more resources built any other way.
- `distributed-representation` — Distributed Representations: Represent a thing with many features that each participate in many things, rather than one dedicated symbol per thing — combinatorics beats enumeration.
- `local-generalization-limits` — The Limits of Local Generalization: A learner that only assumes "nearby inputs get similar outputs" needs as many examples as there are regions to distinguish — depth and structure buy a way around that wall.
- `manifold-hypothesis` — The Manifold Hypothesis: Real-world high-dimensional data isn't spread evenly through its space — it clusters near a much lower-dimensional surface of "things that actually occur."
- `regularization-as-preference` — Regularization as Preference, Not Constraint: Instead of forbidding a model certain solutions, penalize them softly — express a preference the training data can still override if the evidence is strong enough.
- `representation-learning` — Representation Learning: Let the machine learn the features themselves, not just the mapping from features to output — the representation is the hard part worth automating.

## Seven Languages in Seven Weeks (seven-languages-seven-weeks) — Technology & Data
- `actor-model-concurrency` — The Actor Model for Concurrency: Give every concurrent unit its own private state and let it interact with others only by sending messages, eliminating shared-memory locks entirely.

- `let-it-crash-fault-tolerance` — "Let It Crash" Fault Tolerance: Instead of defensively catching every possible error, let a faulty process die outright and have a separate supervising process restart it clean.

- `logic-programming-declarative-solving` — Logic Programming as Declarative Constraint Solving: State facts and rules about a world and let an inference engine search for values that satisfy them, instead of coding the search yourself.
- `pattern-matching-as-control-flow` — Pattern Matching as a Decision Construct: Branch on the shape of data itself — destructuring and binding variables in the same step — instead of testing values with chained conditionals.
- `prototype-vs-class-objects` — Prototype vs. Class-Based Objects: An object system built on cloning concrete objects, rather than instantiating abstract classes, collapses the class/instance distinction entirely.
