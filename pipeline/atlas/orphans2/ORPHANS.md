## Agile & Software Delivery
- `coach-failure-modes` (coaching-agile-teams) — Coach Failure Modes: Eight named, caricatured personas a well-intentioned coach falls into — the Hub, the Expert, the Nag and others — all traceable to unchecked ego or divided attention.
- `cooperation-vs-collaboration` (coaching-agile-teams) — Cooperation vs. Collaboration: Cooperation is the smooth coordinated sum of individual contributions; collaboration adds emergence, producing ideas no individual member could have produced alone.
- `five-levels-of-conflict` (coaching-agile-teams) — The Five Levels of Conflict: Conflict escalates through five distinguishable levels — from factual problem-solving to all-out "World War" — each with its own language signature and appropriate coach response.
- `better-than-not-doing-it` (learning-agile) — "Better-Than-Not-Doing-It" Results: Adopting agile practices one at a time makes a team better at what it already does well, but leaves its blind spots untouched, capping results at "improved," never "transformed."
- `commitments-not-plans` (learning-agile) — Plans Don't Commit Us, Commitments Commit Us: A written plan is only a record of a promise; treating the document as the source of obligation lets people disown commitments by claiming they never agreed to the plan.
- `fractured-perspective` (learning-agile) — A Fractured Perspective: The same shared practice means something different to each role on a team, and when nobody reconciles those meanings, it quietly reproduces the old conflicts it was meant to fix.
- `methodology-vs-mindset` (learning-agile) — Practices, Methodology, and Mindset: Adopting a way of working has three levels — isolated practices, a coherent methodology, an internalized mindset — and each level unlocks a larger payoff than the one below it.
- `course-correction` (personal-kanban) — Course Correction: Plans are never precise, so continually making small, cheap adjustments as reality unfolds beats treating an original plan as fixed and paying a catastrophic price for drift.
- `future-in-progress-limit` (personal-kanban) — The Future-in-Progress Limit: Capping how many big aspirational goals you actively pursue at once, the same way a WIP limit caps active tasks, because unlimited goal-collecting generates more work than any WIP limit downstream can absorb.
- `metacognitive-mapping` (personal-kanban) — Metacognitive Mapping: Visualizing scattered tasks as a spatial map turns them into a legible narrative of your own decision-making, making it possible to examine not just what you do but why.
- `rhythm-and-slack` (personal-kanban) — Cadence and Slack: Work has a natural, detectable beat, and the open gaps between tasks — slack — are what let that beat absorb variation instead of clogging.
- `task-shape-patterns` (personal-kanban) — Matching Structure to Task Type: Repetitive tasks, long projects, emergencies, and small backlog clutter each break a one-size-fits-all workflow differently, so each needs its own visualization shape rather than one uniform column set.
- `automating-below-the-ui` (specification-by-example) — Automating Below the UI: Drive automated checks through an API, HTTP layer, or controller instead of a browser wherever possible — the interface is usually the slowest, flakiest place to verify business logic.
- `deriving-scope-from-goals` (specification-by-example) — Deriving Scope from Goals: Push back on requirements framed as solutions and derive project scope from the underlying business goal instead, often finding a cheaper answer.
- `specification-in-domain-language` (specification-by-example) — Specification in Domain Language: Specifications written in the business's own vocabulary, not invented testing or implementation jargon, stay readable to everyone and need no translation layer.
- `specifying-collaboratively` (specification-by-example) — Specifying Collaboratively: Requirements written by one role in isolation end up either unreadable or unmaintainable — specifications must be built jointly by business, testing, and development perspectives together.
- `countermeasures-as-hypotheses` (value-stream-mapping) — Countermeasures as Hypotheses: Calling every improvement a "countermeasure" rather than a "solution" keeps it framed as a testable, temporary hypothesis rather than a permanent fix to defend.
- `execution-method-triage` (value-stream-mapping) — Execution Method Triage: Sorting improvements into just-do-it, focused-event, or full-project tracks by their complexity and risk, rather than running every fix through the same process.
- `freed-capacity` (value-stream-mapping) — Freed Capacity: Eliminating non-value-adding work frees people's time, not their jobs — using it for layoffs poisons every future improvement effort.
- `narrow-scope-reveals-macro-simplicity` (value-stream-mapping) — Narrow Scope Reveals Macro Simplicity: Deliberately mapping only one narrow slice of a variable process usually surfaces the same root problems as the whole, so the resulting fix generalizes far past its scope.
- `single-point-value-stream-ownership` (value-stream-mapping) — Single-Point Value Stream Ownership: A cross-functional flow of work needs exactly one named person accountable for its end-to-end performance, because distributed functional ownership means no one is actually responsible.

- `solution-method-mistaken-for-problem` (are-your-lights-on) — Don't Mistake a Solution Method for a Problem Definition: A client's proposed technique (enumerate all combinations, run a linear program) is not the problem itself, and treating it as one hides the real question being asked.

- `human-resource-frame` (reframing-organizations) — The Human Resource Frame: Organizations as extended families whose success depends on the "fit" between individual needs and organizational demands, not on treating people as interchangeable inputs.
- `organization-as-theater` (reframing-organizations) — Organization as Theater: When outcomes are hard to measure, an organization's front-stage performance — not its backstage machinery — is what earns belief, support, and legitimacy.
- `political-frame` (reframing-organizations) — The Political Frame: Organizations as coalitions of groups with enduring, legitimate differences in interest competing for scarce resources and power — not a pathology to be eliminated but a permanent condition to be managed.
- `structural-frame` (reframing-organizations) — The Structural Frame: Organizations as factories whose recurring problems are irreducible trade-offs — clarity versus creativity, autonomy versus interdependence — not one-time design errors to be fixed once.
- `symbolic-frame` (reframing-organizations) — The Symbolic Frame: Organizations as tribes and temples held together by shared myth, ritual, and story — where what an event means matters more than what it accomplishes.

## Systems Thinking & Philosophy
- `simple-rules` (simple-rules) — Simple Rules: A handful of rules-of-thumb, tailored to a specific bottleneck activity, that give concrete guidance while leaving room for judgment.
- `authority-override-in-emergencies` (simplexity) — Authority Override in Emergencies: People abandon their own direct observation of danger in favor of official reassurance and the visible choices of others, even when both are wrong.
- `cyclic-collective-preference` (simplexity) — Cyclic Collective Preference: Aggregating individually consistent preferences across three or more options can produce a group "preference" that contradicts itself — A beats B, B beats C, yet C beats A.
- `precision-targeting-not-broad-effort` (simplexity) — Precision Targeting Over Broad Effort: Where effort and results are radically disproportionate, finding the exact point where a small, targeted intervention unlocks a system beats spreading resources evenly across the problem.
- `quarter-power-scaling` (simplexity) — Quarter-Power Scaling Laws: Metabolism, lifespan, and city growth all bend to the same mass-to-the-three-quarters-power formula, so size alone predicts how living and built systems run.
- `status-complexity-mismatch` (simplexity) — The Status-Complexity Mismatch: Pay, title, and prestige are unreliable proxies for a job's true complexity — the visible trappings of work, not the work itself, decide which jobs we call skilled.
- `future-reality-tree` (thinking-for-a-change) — Future Reality Tree: Before implementing an idea, diagram its full predicted consequence chain — including the bad ones — to test whether it actually delivers what you expect.
- `necessary-condition-thinking` (thinking-for-a-change) — Necessary Condition Thinking: Treating a requirement (must/can't/need) as a belief to be surfaced and tested, rather than a fact, often reveals the barrier was never really there.
- `prerequisite-tree` (thinking-for-a-change) — Prerequisite Tree: Turn a goal that feels impossible into a sequenced set of milestones by naming every obstacle in the way and the intermediate objective that overcomes each one.
- `sufficient-cause-thinking` (thinking-for-a-change) — Sufficient Cause Thinking (Effect–Cause–Effect): Treating one thing's existence as inevitably producing another lets you diagram cause-effect claims and hunt the hidden assumption in the arrow.
- `transition-tree` (thinking-for-a-change) — Transition Tree: An action plan is only as good as the causal chain from each step to the condition it must create before the next step can work — verbalize that chain before acting.
- `bundle-self` (whats-it-all-about) — The Bundle Self: The self may be nothing over and above the collection of thoughts, perceptions, and sensations that make it up — a cart is nothing but its parts, suitably arranged.
- `deflationary-meaning` (whats-it-all-about) — The Deflationary Account of Meaning: "Life is meaningless" almost always smuggles in one narrow sense of meaning (externally sourced) and doesn't follow in the many other senses the word carries.
- `good-of-the-whole` (whats-it-all-about) — The Good of the Whole: A collective — a species, nation, or institution — can be thriving even while none of its individual members are, because what's true of the whole isn't reducible to what's true of its parts.
- `invented-purpose` (whats-it-all-about) — Invented, Not Inherited, Purpose: A purpose we assign or discover for ourselves is not an inferior grade of purpose to one built in by a creator at the design stage.
- `meaning-without-morality` (whats-it-all-about) — Meaning Without Morality: A life's felt meaningfulness and its moral status are separable questions — value-to-the-liver is necessary for a meaningful life but need not be sufficient.
